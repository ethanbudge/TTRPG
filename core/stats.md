# The Four Stats

Everything in The Party — a person, a squad, a starship, a whole faction — is described by the same four stats. A stat is rated **1 to 5**, and the rating is your dice pool when that stat is contested. There are no derived modifiers and no skill list: read the number, pick up that many dice.

| Stat | A person's… | A faction's… |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Physical** | Body: strength, speed, endurance, hands-on skill, violence | Muscle: troops, fleets, infrastructure, territory, industry |
| **Mental** | Mind: reasoning, knowledge, perception, planning, craft | Brain: intelligence networks, technology, doctrine, administration |
| **Emotional** | Presence: charm, empathy, deception, performance, nerve | Face: reputation, propaganda reach, diplomatic capital, public love or fear |
| **Ideological** | Core: willpower, faith, principle, self-mastery, devotion | Soul: shared belief, loyalty of members, cultural gravity, zeal |

Ideological flexes with the campaign. Read it as faith for a religious order, ideology for an insurgency, esprit de corps for a ship's crew, the code for a thieves' guild. Whatever your character would suffer for, that's what it measures.

## Which Stat Applies?

The **approach picks the stat**, not the task. Interrogating a prisoner could be Physical (loom and threaten), Mental (catch the contradictions), Emotional (befriend them), or Ideological (an unshakable presence that makes lying feel pointless). The GM sets stakes accordingly — each approach carries its own flavor of failure.

The same logic runs at faction scale. Breaking a blockade could be Physical (punch through), Mental (find the gap in the patrol pattern), Emotional (turn the ports against it), or Ideological (crews who will run cargo through hell because they believe).

## Starting Arrays

At creation, characters and factions each distribute one of these arrays across the four stats:

| Array | Spread | Feels like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Focused** | 4 / 3 / 2 / 2 | A clear specialty and a solid second suit |
| **Broad** | 4 / 3 / 3 / 1 | Strong lead, wide competence, one true blind spot |
| **Spiky** | 4 / 4 / 2 / 1 | Brilliant twice over, and badly flawed |

Every array carries a 4: starting characters are already good at something. A rating of 4 beats standard opposition (3 dice) about three times in four. Advancement rarely raises stats — mostly it widens what you can do. See [Perks & Advancement](characters/advancement.md).

## What Stats Are Not

- Stats are **not health**. Harm marks your [strain boxes](core/strain-and-conditions.md), never the stat.
- Stats are **not skills**. Specificity comes from Seat features, gifts, and who your character is. A surgeon and a safecracker both roll Mental for their craft — but only one of them can credibly declare the goal "I open this man's chest and fix his heart."
- Stats **don't inflate**. The cap is 5 for a first-session rookie and for the faction that rules a continent. Big organizations don't get bigger dice — they get [resources](faction/resources.md) and [assets](faction/nodes.md#assets).
