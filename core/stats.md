# The Four Stats

Everything in The Party — a person, a squad, a starship, a whole faction — is described by the same four stats. A stat is rated **1 to 5**, and the rating *is* your dice pool when that stat is contested. There are no derived modifiers, no skill lists, no math: read the number, pick up that many dice.

| Stat | A person's… | A faction's… |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Physical** | Body: strength, speed, endurance, hands-on skill, violence | Muscle: troops, fleets, infrastructure, territory, industry |
| **Mental** | Mind: reasoning, knowledge, perception, planning, craft | Brain: intelligence networks, technology, doctrine, administration |
| **Emotional** | Presence: charm, empathy, deception, performance, nerve | Face: reputation, propaganda reach, diplomatic capital, public love or fear |
| **Conviction** | Core: willpower, faith, ideological fire, self-mastery, devotion | Soul: cohesion of belief, loyalty of members, cultural gravity, zeal |

> **Naming note:** the fourth stat is called **Conviction** in this draft. It flexes by campaign — read it as *faith* for a religious order, *ideology* for an insurgency, *esprit de corps* for a ship's crew, *the code* for a thieves' guild. Alternate names considered: Spirit, Creed, Resolve. See [Open Questions](appendix/open-questions.md).

## Which Stat Applies?

The **approach picks the stat**, not the task. Interrogating a prisoner could be Physical (loom and threaten), Mental (catch the contradictions), Emotional (befriend them), or Conviction (an unshakable presence that makes lying feel pointless). The GM sets stakes accordingly — approaches carry their own flavors of failure.

The same logic runs at faction scale. Breaking a rival's blockade could be Physical (punch through), Mental (find the gap in their patrol pattern), Emotional (turn the ports against them), or Conviction (crews who will run cargo through hell because they believe).

## Starting Arrays

At creation, characters and factions each distribute one of these arrays across their four stats:

| Array | Spread | Feels like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Focused** | 4 / 3 / 2 / 2 | A clear specialty and a solid second suit |
| **Balanced** | 3 / 3 / 3 / 2 | Reliable at almost everything |
| **Spiky** | 4 / 4 / 2 / 1 | Brilliant and badly flawed |

A rating of 4 means you beat standard opposition (3 dice) about 3 times in 4 — starting characters are already *good*. Advancement (rarely) raises stats, but mostly widens what you can do: see [Perks & Advancement](characters/advancement.md).

## What Stats Are Not

- Stats are **not health.** Harm marks the [strain track](core/strain-and-conditions.md), never the stat.
- Stats are **not skills.** There is no skill list; specificity comes from class features, gifts, and the fiction of who your character is. A surgeon and a safecracker both roll Mental for their craft — but only one of them can credibly declare the goal "I open this man's chest and fix his heart."
- Stats **don't inflate.** The cap is 5, forever, for a first-session rookie and the faction that rules a continent. Big organizations don't get bigger dice — they get [resources](faction/resources.md), [nodes](faction/nodes.md), and assets.
