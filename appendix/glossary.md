# Glossary

Every term of art in The Party, in plain language. Terms in *italics* have their own entries.

**Advance** — Any permanent improvement bought with *perks*: a new feature, a new gift, a raised stat, or an investment in the *faction*.

**Agenda** — The faction's great goal, expressed as a ladder of *clocks*. Filling the last one ends an era of the campaign.

**Aid** — Helping an ally's roll. One helper may grant one *edge* by saying how they help — and they share the consequences if it goes wrong.

**Asset** — Faction property that can act: a strike team, a spy ring, a gunboat. Has a rating (its dice), a 3-box *track*, and a location.

**Band** — One of four distances: **Contact, Close, Far, Distant**. Sized relative to the arena — a band spans a room in a brawl and a star system in a fleet action.

**Beat** — One round of a *conflict*. Every actor gets one *goal* per beat. How much time a beat covers is set by the *clockspeed*.

**Calling** — Your subclass: one of three styles within each *class*, granting a signature feature.

**Campaign** — A series of connected play sessions telling one long story.

**Channel** — Acting through the faction in a conflict: rolling a faction stat or an *asset's* rating instead of your own dice.

**Character (PC)** — A protagonist created and played by one player. "NPC" is everyone else — played by the *GM*.

**Class** — Your office in the faction: Head, Voice, Hand, Eye, Mind, Heart, or Spine. Describes your organizational role, never your day job.

**Clock** — A circle divided into 4, 6, or 8 segments, tracking progress on anything too big for one roll. Wins tick it; filled means it happens.

**Clockspeed** — How much time one *beat* represents: **Blitz** (seconds), **Operation** (minutes–hours), **Season** (days–weeks), **Era** (months+).

**Cohesion** — The faction's own 5-box *track*: its will to remain one organization. Filling it triggers a *Crisis*.

**Commit** — Spending faction *stock* to add dice to a roll. 1 stock = 1 *edge*, maximum 2 per contest.

**Condition** — A named state — *Blinded, Shaken, Starving* — that imposes one *setback* on any roll it would plausibly hinder. **Hard conditions** additionally forbid a category of action until cleared.

**Conflict** — Structured scene time: *beats*, *goals*, and *contests*, used when several actors urgently want incompatible things. Works identically for a fistfight or a trade war.

**Contest** — Every roll in the game: your dice pool against an opposing pool, higher sum wins, ties go to the acting side at a cost.

**Costly success** — The result of a tied contest: the acting side gets what it wanted, and the GM attaches a real complication.

**Crisis** — What happens when *Cohesion* fills: a schism, mutiny, or purge, played out as a full venture.

**Deepen** — The free upgrade to a feature you already own, earned every third *advance*.

**Downtime** — Each character's personal move during the *faction turn*: recover, prepare, work, scheme, treat, or tend the flame.

**Edge / Setback** — One die added to / removed from a pool. Pools always stay between 1 and 5; extra edges past 5 strip dice from the opposition instead.

**Faction** — The organization all the player characters belong to. Shared by the whole table; has stats, resources, territory, a flaw, and an *agenda*.

**Faction turn** — The 20–30 minute phase between missions when the faction produces, eats, moves supplies, acts, and everyone takes *downtime*.

**Flaw** — A trait that invites trouble. When it genuinely complicates your life, the trouble happens and you earn a *perk*.

**GM (Game Master)** — The player who describes the world, plays every NPC and rival faction, sets *stakes*, and rolls the opposing dice in every contest.

**Gift** — A positive trait open to any character — a body, a knack, a history, a blessing. Grants one narrow benefit.

**Goal** — What you declare on your turn: one sentence describing what you want to be true. The table finds the dice from there.

**Node** — A place where the faction exists: a chapel, safehouse, dry dock, or forum server. Has needs, yields, stock, and a 5-box track.

**Perk** — The game's single currency of growth, spent on rerolls, personal *advances*, or faction advances. Earned through play — especially through your *flaws*.

**Protection tag** — Armor, cover, or its social equivalent: once per scene, negate 1 incoming *strain*.

**Route** — A connection between *nodes* along which *stock* and *assets* move. Can be safe, threatened, or cut.

**Scale tier** — Personal (people), Company (squads, vessels), or Host (armies, fleets). Crossing tiers requires leverage.

**Scar** — A permanent consequence accepted instead of being *taken out*. Changes your character forever and earns a *perk*.

**Scene** — One continuous piece of fiction in one place and time, like a scene in a film — the tavern standoff, the war council, the chase across the rooftops. The GM calls when a scene ends. "Once per scene" abilities and *protection tags* refresh then. A *conflict* usually spans one scene regardless of clockspeed (a Season-speed trade-war exchange is still one scene); a *faction turn* is its own scene for these purposes.

**Session** — One sitting of play, typically 2–4 hours.

**Session zero** — The first gathering, where the table builds the faction and characters together before play begins.

**Stakes** — What happens if the acting side loses a contest — always stated before dice are rolled.

**Stat** — One of four ratings from 1 to 5: **Physical, Mental, Emotional, Conviction**. Your stat *is* your dice pool. Factions have the same four.

**Stock** — One unit of a faction resource, stored at a *node*. Comes in eight types: an internal and external pool for each stat.

**Strain** — The game's wounds: boxes marked on a *track* when you lose a harmful contest. Clears with a real breather.

**Strong win** — Winning a contest by 5 or more: the goal happens *and* the winner picks a boon.

**Taken out** — What happens when a track fills: you're out of the conflict, on the winner's terms — within the stated *stakes*.

**Track** — A short row of boxes measuring how much more something can take. Characters have 5, squads and assets 3, mooks 1–2. Never grows with experience.

**Venture** — A mission: the zoomed-in play between *faction turns*, from a single tense conversation to a multi-session infiltration.

**Zoom** — Changing a conflict's scale mid-stream — duel to riot, boarding action to fleet battle — carrying all strain, conditions, and clocks along.
