# Open Questions

Unresolved design decisions, flagged for the designer. Each lists the draft's current answer and what would change it. Roughly ordered by how much they'd reshape the game.

> An independent review with its own recommendation list — overlapping this page in places, contradicting it in none — lives in [Review & Recommendations](appendix/review-recommendations.md). Read both before the next revision.

## Needs a Decision

1. **The fourth stat's name.** Draft uses **Conviction**; candidates were *Spirit, Creed, Resolve, Devotion*. "Conviction" reads secular-but-deep and skins well across faith/ideology/loyalty — but it's two syllables longer than the other three stats and the only abstract noun. Gut-check it at the table.
2. **Lethality dial default.** Draft: death only when pre-stated stakes say so. Is that the right *default*, or should violent conflicts imply it? Affects how much teeth the Hand's Interpose and the scar system have.
3. **Calling depth: Deepen vs. full trees.** Draft ships 1 signature feature per calling plus the generic [Deepen](characters/advancement.md#deepening) upgrade rule. The D&D-style "build anticipation" itch might want true 3-tier calling trees (≈42 more features to write). Recommendation: playtest with Deepen first; write trees only for the callings players gravitate to.
4. **Perk costs are untested.** The 4/5/8 personal and 2–8 faction costs target one advance per ~2 sessions and deliberately price faction growth cheaper. Playtest for: hoarding (nobody spends), poverty (rerolls eat all income), and freeloading (nobody funds the faction because someone else will). The last one is the design's biggest social risk.
5. **Stock granularity.** Draft stores stock **per node** with cap 5 (distribution decisions = gameplay, sieges work). Cost: eight named pools × several nodes on one sheet. If the faction turn drags in play, fall back to: pooled faction-level stock + a "supplied/unsupplied" flag per node. Keep the per-node model through at least three playtests before deciding — it carries the brief's food-to-the-nodes vision.

## Needs Playtesting

6. **7-player beat pacing.** Goal-based turns are fast, but 7 goals + GM actors per beat is still a lot of table time at Blitz speed. Candidate mitigations if it drags: paired goals (two PCs, one contest) as the default at 6+, or capping significant enemy actors at ~3.
7. **Faction turn length.** Target is 20–30 minutes. The step count is small but Actions (one per player) scales with table size. If it bloats: cap total actions at 4–5 regardless of players and let extra players aid.
8. **Tie frequency feel.** Ties (costly success) occur ~7–9% of rolls — roughly one per scene. Confirm that cadence feels like spice, not tax.
9. **The Hand's Interpose (unlimited).** Powerful and death-spiral-adjacent (Hand takes everything, goes down early). Watch it; a once-per-beat limit is the ready nerf.
10. **Asset autonomy.** Idle assets acting alone (rating vs. difficulty) may generate either delightful texture or GM homework. Watch the bookkeeping.

## Deliberately Deferred

11. **A magic/powers module.** The draft handles the supernatural as fiction + Mind features + kit tags — enough for low-supernatural play. High-magic campaigns (fireballs as combat verbs, precognition vs. the Eye's whole deal) deserve a proper module: probably powers-as-features with a Commit-like personal resource. Not started.
12. **Published-adventure conversion guide.** How to run a bought module inside The Party (answer: turn its antagonist into a rival faction with clocks). Worth a page eventually.
13. **PC-vs-PC and schism play.** Crisis events invite it; the contest engine handles it mechanically (it's symmetric), but there's no social-contract guidance for the table. Needed before Crisis rules see real use.
14. **Multi-faction parties.** The draft assumes all PCs share one faction. Double-agent PCs and two-faction tables are unsupported — deliberately, for now.
15. **Solo/duo play.** The faction sheet could plausibly carry a 1–2 player game (the faction as the "party"). Untested, unwritten.
16. **Character/faction/node sheet PDFs.** The ASCII sheets in these docs should become real handouts once the shapes stabilize.

## Watch-Items From the Research

- **Band of Blades' warning:** its reviewers loved the role-sheets but found the *many separate subsystems* crowded out roleplay. The Party's counter-bet is radical reuse (one engine, one track shape, one condition rule). If any future addition needs its own resolution mechanic, that's the alarm bell.
- **Daggerheart's warning:** GM-resource pacing (Fear) can push GMs toward hoarding and repetitive moves. The Party avoided a GM metacurrency entirely — costs come from fiction and ties. If a future draft adds one, re-read those reviews first.
