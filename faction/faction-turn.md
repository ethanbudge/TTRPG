# The Faction Turn

The faction turn is the organizational heartbeat: the faction earns, pays, acts, and every character gets personal downtime. It takes twenty to thirty minutes, played together.

**It runs when the GM lets time pass** — after a job, over a journey, at the turn of a season. There is no required cadence. A breathless arc might go three sessions without one; a slow winter might run two back to back. When the story exhales, the machine turns. What the turn *represents* stretches with it: a faction turn after a frantic week covers that week; one after a quiet half-year covers the half-year.

Deal the steps out around the table. The player whose Seat matches a step is its natural owner, but anyone can run anything — the turn works the same with seven players or three, two Spines or none.

## The Four Steps

### 1 · Income

Each holding and enterprise produces per its Gives line. Add the stock to the Ledger, noting where it lands. Seat synergies from doubled Seats land here. While any [hunger condition](faction/resources.md#going-hungry) holds, its income line produces 1 less.

### 2 · Keep

The faction's needs come due: the Keep is the short list of what existing costs — who eats, who gets paid, what must stay believed. Pay each line from stock that can plausibly reach it (Ready or Reachable from where the need lives). Can't pay, or won't? [Going Hungry](faction/resources.md#going-hungry). Pay everything with stock to spare and the faction is **Flourishing** until next turn.

*Income lands first, then hunger. The drama of most turns is decided right here — who eats?*

**Time also moves the map at this step:** Remote stock becomes Reachable, Reachable becomes Ready where the faction means to use it, unless it's [Cut Off](faction/nodes.md#accessibility).

### 3 · Actions

The faction takes **one action per player** (+1 per Head, max +2). Each action is led by one player who describes how their character drives it, then rolled as a normal [contest](core/contests.md) with a faction stat — plus edges from Seat synergies, committed stock, and the situation.

Actions are goal-based like everything else. This menu is examples, not walls:

| Action | Typical stat | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Put Down Roots** | varies | Start or strike a new-holding countdown (4 segments) |
| **Develop** | Physical / Mental | Build an asset, start a project countdown, improve a holding |
| **Campaign** | Emotional / Ideological | Spread influence: strike agenda or regional countdowns, pin conditions on rivals (usually burns 1 Influence or Zeal) |
| **Strike** | Physical | Hit a rival's holding, cache, or asset (usually burns 1 Force) — big strikes deserve a full [faction conflict](combat/faction.md) |
| **Spy / Sabotage** | Mental | Steal intelligence, cut a rival's stock off, pin conditions quietly |
| **Trade** | Emotional / Mental | Swap stock 2:1 at market, 1:1 with Allies; move stock the long way |
| **Quell / Tend** | Emotional / Ideological | Clear faction conditions, restore 1 Cohesion |
| **Recruit** | Ideological | Gain 1 stock of Zeal or Force (2 on a Triumph) |

Against a rival, the GM rolls the rival's stat; against the world, a difficulty pool. Stock committed to an action is spent win or lose — the pamphlets got printed either way.

**Big swings deserve screen time.** The GM should regularly answer an ambitious faction action with *"that's a mission — do you want to play it?"* The faction turn is for the machine; the table is for the story.

### 4 · Downtime

Each character takes one downtime move:

| Move | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Recover** | Clear your lasting conditions; put the last job down. |
| **Treat** | Clear another's lasting conditions — or sit with someone's scar-flaw: it doesn't bite for the rest of this arc. |
| **Prepare** | Bank one primed edge for the coming job. |
| **Work** | Add 1 stock (any type your story can justify) to the place you're at. |
| **Scheme** | Start or strike a personal countdown — the revenge, the romance, the secret. |
| **Tend the Flame** | Time with the faction's people: restore 1 Cohesion, or clear one condition carried by the faction's people where you are. |

Downtime is also the natural moment to spend perks — and to ask the question the game keeps asking. The evening you spend practicing your swordwork is an evening the recruits drilled without you.

## Then the Rivals Move

After the players' turn, each [rival faction](faction/rivals.md) takes one action toward its current goal, and the GM ticks whatever the world advanced on its own. Do it visibly when the narrative allows — rumors, headlines, troop movements. The map should never feel like it waits for the players.

For tables of six or more players, give major rivals two actions. Eight player actions against one is not a rivalry; it's target practice.
