# Rival Factions

The world is full of organizations that want what yours wants. Rivals use the same anatomy as the players' faction, compressed to what the GM actually needs at the table.

Rivals don't keep an eight-pool Ledger — that bookkeeping belongs to the protagonist faction only. A rival's economy is abstracted into its stats, assets, and the GM's notes. If the players choke off a rival's supplies, express it as conditions (*Starving*, *Overextended*) and snags, like [everywhere else](core/strain-and-conditions.md#conditions-at-faction-scale).

## The Ladder

Every rival (and notable neutral) sits on the **relationship ladder**:

| Position | Stance | At the table |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **+3 Allied** | Bound to you | Lends stock or an asset (1 per faction turn); joins your conflicts when the cause is shared |
| **+2 Friendly** | Wants you to win | Trades 1:1; shares intelligence; warns you |
| **+1 Warm** | Well-disposed | Small favors; benefit of the doubt |
| **0 Neutral** | Indifferent | Deals at market rates |
| **−1 Cold** | Wary of you | Watches you; charges you more; talks behind your back |
| **−2 Hostile** | Wants you to lose | Spends actions against you when convenient |
| **−3 At War** | Committed | Spends actions against you even when it hurts them |

The ladder moves through the story, not through a mechanic: broken deals, public humiliations, weddings, funerals, rescues. The GM announces shifts openly — factions telegraph their feelings through emissaries, editorials, and troop movements.

## Running Rival Turns

After each player faction turn, give each rival one action toward its current goal (two for major rivals at big tables), then tick what the world advanced on its own. Guidelines:

- **One roll or none.** If a rival's action doesn't touch the players' faction, don't roll — strike their countdown and narrate rumor. Roll only at points of collision.
- **Telegraph, then hit.** A rival countdown should be visible by its halfway mark — informants, headlines, sermons. Players deserve one faction turn of warning before most hammers fall.
- **Rivals fight each other.** Two or three rivals with incompatible causes generate more plot than any prepared adventure. The players' faction is one wolf in a wood of wolves.
- **Rivals have flaws too.** Give each one an exploitable dysfunction, and let the Eye find it.

What rivals do to you between turns: **raid** a cache (steal or spoil stock), **cut off** stock or an asset (a blockade, a seizure, a watched road), **watch** your movements (their next action against you gains an edge), or strike at a holding — which defends itself ([Defending What's Yours](faction/nodes.md#defending-whats-yours)).

## Scaling Difficulty

The rival roster is the campaign's difficulty dial:

- Early arcs: rivals with stats 2–3, sloppy assets, visible countdowns.
- Later arcs: stats 4–5, layered assets, hidden countdowns, and — most dangerous — rivals who cooperate against you.

A veteran faction doesn't face bigger dice. It faces smarter webs.
