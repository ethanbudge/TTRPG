# Moments of Play

One small, complete example for each working part of The Party — each from a different table, a different genre, a different mood. Every moment links to the chapter that governs it.

## Declaring a Goal — [Contests](core/contests.md)

<div class="callout example" data-title="Aboard the free trader Vindicta — spacefaring">
<p><span class="speaker">Theo (Yara, the Head):</span> "I fire the docking thrusters manually."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Okay — toward what? What do you want to be true?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Theo:</span> "Right. Goal: I want us wedged into the wreck's shadow before their sensor sweep comes around."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Now <em>that</em> I can set stakes for."</p>
</div>

The first sentence was a button-press; the second is a goal. The GM's question — *what do you want to be true?* — is the most-used tool in the game.

## Setting Stakes — [Contests](core/contests.md#the-contest-procedure)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The catacombs beneath Vael — fantasy">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "You can absolutely try to translate the binding-script by candlelight. Stakes: if you lose, you'll get a working translation anyway — tomorrow. And tonight, the thing in the wall gets to act first. Roll Mental against 4."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Ines:</span> "So failure costs me <em>time</em>, not the answer. That's worse. Rolling."</p>
</div>

Stakes are stated before dice. Notice the loss isn't "nothing happens" — it's the situation getting away from her.

## The Tie — [Costly Success](core/contests.md#outcomes)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A divorce lawyer's office, after hours — modern heist">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Your 12, my 12. Tie — you're the one acting, so you get it: the file drawer's open, the folder's in your jacket. But the cost: as you slide the drawer shut, headlights sweep the parking lot. Someone came back to the office."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dana:</span> "I hate this game. Don't stop."</p>
</div>

Ties always move the story forward and always leave a bruise — about one roll in twelve lands here.

## The Strong Win — [Boons](core/contests.md#outcomes)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A rooftop over Ministry Square — steampunk">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "19 against my 11 — that's a strong win, take a boon: extra strain on the spotter team, a better position, tick the uprising clock, pin a condition, or shrug off a strain of your own."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Rafe:</span> "Position. When the flare goes up, I want to already be inside their signal tower."</p>
</div>

## Conditions Change the Fight — [Strain & Conditions](core/strain-and-conditions.md#conditions)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The Grinning Lantern's cellar — fantasy">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Ossler's <em>Hesitant</em> now — that's a setback on anything he does to press forward. He can still fight; he just can't make himself take the stairs. So he does the smart thing instead and starts shouting for crossbows."</p>
</div>

A condition isn't a stun — it's the fiction growing a fact that every later roll has to respect.

## The Upkeep Pinch — [The Faction Turn](faction/faction-turn.md#2--upkeep)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The Vindicta's mess hall — spacefaring">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Upkeep. The ship needs 1 Provisions — air scrubber filters. The forward hold has none; your filters are sitting at the Meridian Station cache, one cut route away, thanks to the blockade."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya (the Spine):</span> "I have <em>Supply Lines Hold</em>. The convoy got through after all — I'm spending it. A courier skiff, flying cold, no transponder. She docks at 0400 and says nothing."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "The scrubbers hum on. That's your once-per-turn — hope nothing else is starving."</p>
</div>

## The Perk Dilemma — [Advancement](characters/advancement.md)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A courtyard at dusk — any genre, every genre">
<p><span class="speaker">Dev (Brakk, the Hand):</span> "I've got four perks banked. I could finally take <em>Terror of the Field</em>…"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "The recruits are drilling in the courtyard below your window. Badly."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "…or three perks trains them into an actual strike team asset. Ugh. The whole guild fights better, or <em>I</em> do."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Maya:</span> "The guild watched you spend the last one on yourself."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "The strike team. Fine. But I'm naming it Brakk's Own."</p>
</div>

This choice — practice your swordwork or train the recruits — is the advancement system. It never stops being a real decision.

## Zooming Out — [Conflict](combat/overview.md#zooming)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A duel on the courthouse steps — steampunk">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Steel rings on the marble — and the crowd doesn't disperse. They surge. Someone's thrown a Ministry constable into the fountain. This isn't a duel anymore; it's the start of a riot. We're zooming out: faction rules are live. Your strain and his <em>Winded</em> condition carry straight over. What's the Movement doing with this crowd?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Lena (the Voice):</span> "Committing 1 Zeal. These people know the songs. I <em>taught</em> them the songs."</p>
</div>
