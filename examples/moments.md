# Moments of Play

One short, complete example for each working part of The Party — each from a different table, a different genre, a different mood. Every moment links to the chapter that governs it.

## Declaring a Goal — [Contests](core/contests.md)

<div class="callout example" data-title="Aboard the free trader Vindicta — spacefaring">
<p><span class="speaker">Theo (Yara, the Head):</span> "I fire the docking thrusters manually."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Okay — toward what? What do you want to be true?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Theo:</span> "Right. Goal: I want us wedged into the wreck's shadow before their sensor sweep comes around."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "<em>That</em> I can set stakes for."</p>
</div>

The first sentence was a button-press; the second is a goal. *What do you want to be true?* is the most-used question in the game.

## Setting Stakes — [Contests](core/contests.md#the-contest-procedure)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The catacombs beneath Vael — fantasy">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "You can absolutely try to translate the binding-script by candlelight. Stakes: if you lose, you'll get a working translation anyway — tomorrow. And tonight, the thing in the wall gets to act first. Roll Mental against 4."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Ines:</span> "So failure costs me <em>time</em>, not the answer. That's worse. Rolling."</p>
</div>

Stakes come before dice, and a loss is never "nothing happens" — it's the situation getting away from you.

## The Tie — [Costly Success](core/contests.md#outcomes)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A divorce lawyer's office, after hours — modern heist">

<p><span class="speaker">Dana:</span> "I hate this game. Don't stop."</p>
</div>

## The Triumph — [Boons](core/contests.md#outcomes)

<div class="callout example" data-title="A rooftop over Ministry Square — steampunk">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "19 against my 11 — that's a Triumph. Take a boon: extra strain on the spotter team, a better position, a segment off the uprising countdown, a condition pinned, or shrug off a strain of your own."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Rafe:</span> "Position. When the flare goes up, I want to already be inside their signal tower."</p>
</div>

## Conditions Change the Fight — [Strain & Conditions](core/strain-and-conditions.md#conditions)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The Grinning Lantern's cellar — fantasy">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Ossler's <em>Hesitant</em> now — that's a snag on anything he does to press forward. He can still fight; he just can't make himself take the stairs. So he does the smart thing instead and starts shouting for crossbows."</p>
</div>

A condition isn't a stun. It's the situation growing a fact that every later roll has to respect.

## The Ledger and the Keep — [Resources](faction/resources.md) & [Accessibility](faction/nodes.md#accessibility)

<div class="callout example" data-title="The Vindicta's mess hall — spacefaring">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Faction turn — the Keep comes due. The ship asks 1 Provisions: air scrubber filters. The Ledger says your filters sit in the Meridian Station cache, and Meridian is Cut Off since the blockade closed."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya (the Spine):</span> "<em>Supply Lines Hold.</em> I'm rolling Mental against the blockade — call it 3." <em>Rolls: 14 against 9.</em> "A courier skiff, flying cold, no transponder. She docks at 0400 and says nothing."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "The scrubbers hum on. That's your once-per-turn — hope nothing else is starving."</p>
</div>

## Zooming Out — [Conflict](combat/overview.md#zooming)

This isn't a duel anymore; it's the start of a riot. Zooming out: faction rules are live, and your strain and his <em>Winded</em> carry straight over. What's the Movement doing with this crowd?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Lena (the Mouth):</span> "Committing 1 Zeal. These people know the songs. I <em>taught</em> them the songs."</p>
</div>
