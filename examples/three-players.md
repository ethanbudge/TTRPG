# Three Players and a Throne

A small table running **the Crooked Mitre** ([genre frame](gm/genre-frames.md#the-crooked-mitre--a-medieval-religion-takes-a-kingdom)) — a heretical order maneuvering its faith toward the crown of Vael. Three players, no [Hand](characters/classes/hand.md), no [Eye](characters/classes/eye.md), and a campaign about why they don't need them.

*At the table: the GM · Ines as **Abbess Rochelle** (Head, Sovereign) · Marcus as **Friar Talbot** (Voice, Herald) · Ana as **Sister Wren** (Spine, Steward).*

## A Social Conflict at Blitz Speed

The order's rival, Cardinal Osmund, has called a surprise conclave to brand their teachings heresy. The GM frames it as a true [conflict](combat/overview.md) — clockspeed Blitz, arena the conclave floor, and everyone's **strain is standing**: five boxes of credibility, marked like any wound. A filled track here means being shouted from the hall.

<div class="callout example" data-title="Beat one — three goals, three minutes">
<p><span class="speaker">Marcus (Talbot):</span> "Goal — before Osmund even speaks, I want the gallery crowd murmuring our psalm. The one we seeded in the almshouses." <em>Emotional 4, plus an edge — the faction committed 1 stock of</em> the Love of the Crowds. <em>Five dice: 21 against 13.</em> <strong>Strong win.</strong> "Boon: tick the conclave clock."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "The gallery hums the psalm under Osmund's opening curse. He visibly loses the room's edges. Rochelle — he wheels on you: 'Will the abbess deny her order shelters heretics?' He's initiating — his Emotional 4 against yours."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Ines (Rochelle):</span> "I don't deny it. Goal: I want his accusation to become <em>my</em> platform — 'yes, we shelter whom the crown abandons.' Conviction, not Emotional. I'm not charming anyone; I'm bearing witness." <em>Conviction 4 vs Osmund's 4: 15–15. Tie — but Osmund was acting.</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Costly success, his way: the hall gasps — the accusation lands, you ARE branded shelterers of heretics. His cost: you chose the branding, wore it like a mitre. Mark 1 strain, Rochelle — and take <em>Martyr's Halo</em> as a condition on Osmund's side of the fiction: everything he does against you now looks like persecution."</p>
</div>

<div class="callout" data-title="What to notice">
<p>The "fight" used the exact rules of a sword fight — beats, goals, strain, conditions — reskinned to a conclave floor, per <a href="#/combat/individual?id=duels-of-every-kind">Duels of Every Kind</a>. And a tie produced the session's best scene, which is what ties are for.</p>
</div>

## The Faction Turn, Short Three Hands

With three players, the [faction turn](faction/faction-turn.md) runs three actions (+1 for Rochelle's Head synergy = four). No Eye means nobody has an edge on Spy actions — it does not mean nobody spies.

<div class="callout example" data-title="Working without an Eye">
<p><span class="speaker">Ana (Wren):</span> "Fourth action: I want eyes inside Osmund's household. No Eye at this table, so no class edge — I'll do it Wren's way. Goal: place a tithe-auditor in his kitchen accounts. Faction Mental 3, plus 1 stock of</em> Confessional Secrets <em>committed — his cook owes us a quiet debt. Four dice against his Mental 4."</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Rolls 17 against 14 — the auditor is in. You don't get an Eye's tricks, but you get what the dice say: a source. I'm starting a clock, <em>Osmund's Ledger Opens</em>, at 4, one segment filled."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Marcus:</span> "Missing classes just means we do it with our own hands. Slower, weirder, more us."</p>
</div>

## The Downtime Question

The turn ends with each player taking one [downtime move](faction/faction-turn.md#5--downtime) — and the session's real cliffhanger isn't the conclave; it's Ana announcing she's saving toward founding a new node (5 perks): a poorhouse-priory inside the capital walls. Ines points out those perks could raise faction Conviction instead. The table adjourns arguing about it, which is the game working exactly as intended.
