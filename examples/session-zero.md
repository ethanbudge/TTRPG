# Session Zero: Founding the Undercroft

Four friends and a GM build a campaign from nothing in about ninety minutes, following the [faction creation steps](faction/overview.md#faction-creation-session-zero). This is the table whose scenes appear throughout the core rules — the night Sable, Brakk, Vess, and Mother Halla first put dice on the table.

*At the table: the GM, Maya, Dev, Priya, and Sam.*

## Picking the Frame and the Cause

<div class="callout example" data-title="Steps 1–2 · frame and cause">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Votes are in — fantasy city, thieves' guild. The frame's called <a href="#/gm/genre-frames?id=the-undercroft--a-thieves-guild-in-a-fantasy-city">the Undercroft</a>. So: the cause. One sentence, with a verb. Why does this guild exist?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "Steal everything?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Boring. What if we're the ones keeping the city's poor districts alive? Like — the crown taxes the docks to death, and we're the leak in the pipe."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "'The Undercroft will own the harbor the crown bled dry — and feed it.' Robin Hood with ledgers."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Written. Notice you just picked half your enemies."</p>
</div>

## Stats and the Eight Pools

The table takes the **Balanced array** (3/3/3/2) for the faction — a guild that does everything competently — putting the 2 in Physical: this guild wins by wit and love, not muscle. Then the most important step: naming the [eight pools](faction/resources.md).

<div class="callout example" data-title="Step 4 · naming the pools">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Internal Physical. What does this guild literally eat?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Bread and bribes. Call it <em>Safehouse Bread</em>."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "External Physical — the muscle you project?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "<em>Knives in the Dark</em>. Obviously."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "External Emotional is <em>Fear With Your Name On It</em> — no wait. Our cause is feeding people. It's <em>The Harbor's Love</em>. Fear is what the <strong>Consortium</strong> projects."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> <em>(writing it down)</em> "You realize you just designed the whole campaign's argument, right? Their fear versus your love, ledger versus ledger."</p>
</div>

The full grid, five minutes later: *Safehouse Bread / Knives in the Dark* (Physical), *Apprenticeships / Every Lock's Secret* (Mental), *The Family Feeling / The Harbor's Love* (Emotional), *Guild Law / The Oath* (Conviction).

## Nodes, Stock, and the Flaw

Three starting [nodes](faction/nodes.md): the **Undercroft cellars** (stronghold — needs Bread and the Family Feeling, yields Guild Law and Lock-Secrets), the **Grinning Lantern** (dockside tavern and listening post), and **Wren's pawnshop** (the fence). Routes: tunnels between cellars and Lantern (safe), rooftop highway to the pawnshop (safe, for now). They seed their 8 starting stock heavy on Bread and the Family Feeling — a guild that feeds its own first.

<div class="callout" data-title="The GM checks the ledger">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Before we lock this — look at your grid. Nothing you own <em>yields</em> Bread. Every turn, your people eat, and every turn, that Bread comes from jobs, Trade actions, or somebody's downtime. Deliberate?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Deliberate. A thieves' guild that has to steal to eat? That's not a bug, that's the premise."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Then it's beautiful. But if the jobs dry up, the shortfall rules do not care how thematic you are."</p>
<p>New tables: check Needs against Yields at creation on purpose. A deficit is a fine engine for play — an <em>accidental</em> deficit is a starved stronghold by turn three.</p>
</div>

For the faction [flaw](characters/traits.md#the-faction-has-one-too), the table debates *Indebted* versus *Soft-Hearted* and lands on writing their own: **"Never Turns Away a Hungry Mouth."** The GM smiles in a way nobody likes.

## The Officers

<div class="callout example" data-title="Character choices, around the table">
<p><span class="speaker">Maya:</span> "Sable. <a href='#/characters/classes/eye'>Eye</a>, Shadow calling — second-story artist, casing the Consortium's counting house since before it was cool. Focused array, 4 in Physical. Flaw: <em>Wanted</em>."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "Brakk, <a href='#/characters/classes/hand'>Hand</a>, Warden. Ex-harbor watchman who couldn't stomach the evictions. Gift: <em>Big</em>. Flaw: <em>Soft Heart</em> — which, given our faction flaw, is going to be a problem."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "Vess, <a href='#/characters/classes/voice'>Voice</a>, Envoy. She talks to harbormasters, magistrates, and worse. Day job: divorce solicitor. Office: the guild's silver tongue."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Mother Halla. <a href='#/characters/classes/heart'>Heart</a>, Shepherd. She runs the soup line out of the Undercroft — she IS the faction flaw. Spiky array: Conviction 4, Emotional 4, and a 1 in Physical she intends to make everyone else's problem."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "No Head, no Spine, no Mind. You know that's fine, right? Anyone can lead a faction action — you'll just feel where the style is missing."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "We're a guild with no treasurer and no boss. That's not a gap, that's a <em>premise</em>."</p>
</div>

## The Oath

The session ends with step nine — each player answers: *what does the faction ask of your character that they're not sure they can give?* Sable: her name off the wanted boards, someday, which the guild can never safely arrange. Brakk: violence, again, in a cause worth it this time. Vess: honesty with people she loves. Halla: the willingness to let one mouth go hungry for the good of the rest — the one thing she's sworn never to do.

<div class="callout" data-title="What to notice">
<p>Every mechanical choice doubled as a story choice: the pools named the campaign's themes, the flaw wrote the GM's job description, the missing classes became a premise. Session zero in The Party isn't setup before the game — it's the first session <em>of</em> the game.</p>
</div>
