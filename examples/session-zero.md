# Session Zero: Founding the Undercroft

Four friends and a GM build a campaign from nothing in about ninety minutes, following the [faction creation steps](faction/overview.md#faction-creation-session-zero). This is the table whose scenes appear throughout the core rules — the night Sable, Brakk, Vess, and Mother Halla first put dice on the table.

*At the table: the GM, Maya, Dev, Priya, and Sam.*

## The Frame and the Cause

<div class="callout example" data-title="Steps 1–2 · frame and cause">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Votes are in — fantasy city, thieves' guild. The frame's called <a href="#/gm/genre-frames?id=the-undercroft--a-thieves-guild-in-a-fantasy-city">the Undercroft</a>. So: the cause. One sentence, with a verb. Why does this guild exist?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "Steal everything?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Boring. What if we're the ones keeping the poor districts alive? The crown taxes the docks to death, and we're the leak in the pipe."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "'The Undercroft will own the harbor the crown bled dry — and feed it.' Robin Hood with ledgers."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Written. You realize you just picked half your enemies."</p>
</div>

## Stats and the Eight Pools

For the faction's array the table takes **Broad** (4/3/3/1): Emotional 4, Mental 3, Ideological 3 — and the 1 in Physical. This guild wins by wit and love, not muscle, and now the sheet says so. Then the naming of the [eight pools](faction/resources.md):

<div class="callout example" data-title="Step 4 · naming the pools">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Internal Physical. What does this guild literally eat?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Bread and bribes. Call it <em>Safehouse Bread</em>."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "External Physical — the muscle you project?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "<em>Knives in the Dark</em>. Obviously."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "External Emotional is <em>Fear With Your Name On It</em> — no, wait. Our cause is feeding people. It's <em>The Harbor's Love</em>. Fear is what the <strong>Consortium</strong> projects."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> <em>(writing)</em> "Their fear against your love. That's the campaign, right there."</p>
</div>

The full grid, five minutes later: *Safehouse Bread / Knives in the Dark* (Physical), *Apprenticeships / Every Lock's Secret* (Mental), *The Family Feeling / The Harbor's Love* (Emotional), *Guild Law / The Oath* (Ideological).

## The Map, the Ledger, and the Keep

Three holdings go on the map: the **Undercroft cellars** (stronghold — gives Guild Law and Lock-Secrets; asks Safehouse Bread), the **Grinning Lantern** (dockside tavern and listening post — gives Intelligence and the Harbor's Love; asks Bread), and **Wren's pawnshop** (the fence — gives Bread when trade is good; asks nothing but discretion). The 8 starting stock lands heavy on Bread and the Family Feeling, split between the cellars and the Lantern — a guild that feeds its own first, and knows where every loaf sits.

Then the Keep — what existing costs, every faction turn — and the first hard look at the books:

<div class="callout example" data-title="Step 6 · the GM checks the ledger">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Before we lock this: your Keep says the cellars and the Lantern eat 2 Bread a turn, and the soup line eats another. Look at your income lines. Almost nothing you own <em>produces</em> Bread. Every turn, that Bread comes from jobs, Trade actions, or somebody's downtime. Deliberate?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Deliberate. A thieves' guild that has to steal to eat? That's not a bug, that's the premise."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Then it's beautiful. But the hunger rules won't care how thematic you are."</p>
<p>New tables: check your Keep against your income on purpose. A deficit is a fine engine for play. An <em>accidental</em> deficit is a starved stronghold by the third turn.</p>
</div>

For the faction's traits, the table takes the gift **Beloved** — the harbor protects what feeds it — and skins the flaw **Overextended** as *"Never Turns Away a Hungry Mouth"*: the Keep always includes one need the income can't cover, and it's the soup line. The GM smiles in a way nobody likes.

## The Officers

<div class="callout example" data-title="Character choices, around the table">
<p><span class="speaker">Maya:</span> "Sable. <a href='#/characters/classes/eye'>Eye</a>, Seeker calling — she's had the Consortium's counting house cased since before it was fashionable, and she reads a room like a ledger. Focused array, the 4 in Mental. Flaw: <em>Wanted</em>."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "Brakk, <a href='#/characters/classes/fist'>Fist</a>, Warden. Ex-harbor watchman who couldn't stomach the evictions. Gifts: <em>Big</em> and <em>Iron Frame</em>. Flaw: <em>Soft Heart</em> — which, given the soup line, is going to be a problem."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Priya:</span> "Vess, <a href='#/characters/classes/mouth'>Mouth</a>, Envoy. She talks to harbormasters, magistrates, and worse. Day job: divorce solicitor. Flaw: <em>Proud</em> — she has never once lost an argument gracefully."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sam:</span> "Mother Halla. <a href='#/characters/classes/heart'>Heart</a>, Shepherd. She runs the soup line out of the cellars — she <em>is</em> the faction flaw. Spiky array: Ideological 4, Emotional 4, Mental 2, and a Physical 1 she intends to make everyone else's problem. Flaw: <em>Soft Heart</em>, obviously."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "No Head, no Spine, no Mind. You know that's fine, right? Anyone can lead a faction action — you'll just feel where the style is missing."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Dev:</span> "A guild with no treasurer and no boss. That's not a gap, that's a <em>premise</em>."</p>
</div>

## First Job

The GM sets the rivals — the Gilded Consortium at Cold, the Watch at Hostile — and closes the night the way session zeros should close: with work.

<div class="callout example" data-title="The hook">
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "It's the third week of autumn. The Consortium's grain barge <em>Providence</em> sits at the customs wharf overnight, hold full, tariff-sealed. Your Keep comes due in four days, and Halla's line is a hundred mouths long. Sable — you cased that wharf two jobs ago. What did you tell them?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Maya:</span> "That the seal is wax, and wax melts."</p>
</div>

<div class="callout" data-title="Worth noticing">
<p>Every mechanical choice doubled as a story choice: the pools named the campaign's themes, the Keep wrote the GM's job for him, and the missing Seats became a premise. Session zero isn't setup before the game. It's the first session of it.</p>
</div>
