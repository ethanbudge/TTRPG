# Examples in Play

Rules say what's legal. Examples show what it feels like. This section is a stack of scenes from tables playing The Party — different genres, group sizes, and moods, with dice totals and table-talk included.

If you've never played a roleplaying game, start here rather than with the rules. You'll absorb most of the engine by eavesdropping.

## How to Read the Transcripts

- <span class="speaker">Small caps names</span> are people at the table. The **GM** runs the world; everyone else plays one character.
- Dice are shown as totals — *"rolls 4 dice: 16"* — because that's all the table ever needs.
- Commentary boxes point at the rules in play, with links to the chapters that govern them.

One campaign — the **Undercroft** thieves' guild, its taproom listening post the **Grinning Lantern**, and its dockside enemy the **Gilded Consortium** — also runs through the worked examples in the core rules ([a fight](combat/individual.md#worked-example--the-grinning-lantern-goes-loud), [a contest](core/contests.md#the-contest-procedure), [a rival sheet](faction/rivals.md#the-rival-sheet)). The [session zero example](examples/session-zero.md) is the night that campaign began.
