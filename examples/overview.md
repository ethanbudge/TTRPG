# Examples in Play

Rules tell you what's legal. Examples tell you what it *feels like*. This section is a stack of scenes from imaginary tables — different genres, different group sizes, different moods — showing The Party being actually played, dice totals and table-talk included.

If you're new to roleplaying games entirely, start here, not with the rules. You'll absorb the whole engine by eavesdropping.

## How to Read the Transcripts

- <span class="speaker">Small caps names</span> are people at the table. The **GM** runs the world; everyone else plays one character.
- Dice are shown as totals — *"rolls 4 dice: 16"* — because that's all the table ever needs.
- Commentary boxes point at what just happened rules-wise, with links into the chapters that govern it.

One running campaign — the **Undercroft** thieves' guild, its taproom listening post the **Grinning Lantern**, and its dockside enemy the **Gilded Consortium** — also appears in worked examples throughout the core rules ([a fight](combat/individual.md#worked-example--the-grinning-lantern-goes-loud), [a contest](core/contests.md#the-contest-procedure), [a rival sheet](faction/rivals.md#the-rival-sheet)). The [session zero example](examples/session-zero.md) shows the night that campaign was born.
