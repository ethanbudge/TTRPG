# Seven at the Barricade

A full seven-player table running **Smoke & Brass** ([genre frame](gm/genre-frames.md#smoke--brass--a-steampunk-insurgency)) — the night the Movement decides to hold Fettler's Junction against the Ministry of Order. This is a [faction conflict](combat/faction.md) with everything switched on: all four resource moves, duplicate classes, mixed [scale tiers](combat/faction.md#scale), and the pacing tools that keep a big table fast.

*At the table: the GM · Lena, **Voice** (Herald) · Rafe, **Eye** (Seeker) · Old Marta, **Spine** (Quartermaster) · Odile, **Heart** (Kindler) · Pieter, **Head** (Firstblade) · and two Hands — Cass (Champion) and Brand (Breaker) — because when the frame is "insurgency," two players want to swing hammers, and the game is built for that.*

*On the field: the Movement's militia cells (asset, Force 3, track 3, Sweeping) and the barricade itself. Against them: Ministry constable companies (Company tier, pool 3) and the **Ironshod** — an armored steam-walker (Company tier, Physical 4, track 3, protection 1, Anchored when its boiler is cold). Objective clocks: <strong>Hold Until Dawn (6)</strong> against the Ministry's <strong>Break the Junction (6)</strong>.*

## Keeping Seven Beats Fast

<div class="callout" data-title="Big-table pacing, stated out loud">
<p>The GM opens with two table rules from the <a href="#/gm/running?id=pacing-the-session">GM Guide</a>: goals are one sentence, and paired goals are encouraged — two players, one contest, shared stakes. Seven players resolve a beat in about ten minutes. The GM runs only three opposition actors: the constables (as one), the Ironshod, and the Ministry's unseen commander (who acts through clock ticks).</p>
</div>

## Beat One — the Line Holds

<div class="callout example" data-title="Paired goals and the four resource moves">
<p><span class="speaker">Cass + Brand (paired goal):</span> "We're the wall. Goal: the first constable rush breaks on us and <em>knows it</em>." <em>One contest — Cass rolls Physical 4 +1 (Brand's aid) vs constables' 3: 17–10, strong win. Constables mark 2, and take</em> Shaken <em>as the boon.</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">Old Marta (Spine):</span> "My goal is a set-up: hot soup moving down the line all night — I want this militia fed, warm, and unshakeable when the push comes. I'm Committing 1 stock of Bread &amp; Coal to my own roll, and yes, GM, the stock is <em>here</em>: I moved it along the sewer route last faction turn, on purpose, because I read the rules." <em>Faction Emotional 3 +1 stock = 4 vs difficulty 2: win. Marta banks one edge for a named ally — Odile — instead of taking effect.</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">Pieter (Head, Firstblade):</span> "Goal: I'm on the barricade crest where everyone can see me. <em>Chain of Command</em> — Rafe, act now: find me the Ironshod's timing."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Rafe (Eye, Seeker):</span> "<em>Pattern Recognition</em> — GM, show me its pool before I commit." <em>The GM reveals: 4 dice, +1 edge while its boiler runs hot.</em> "Then my goal: I want the minute its stokers rest. Everyone will know when to hit it."</p>
</div>

## Beat Two — the Walker

The Ironshod comes on. Company tier against Personal-tier heroes: on the walker's win it marks 2 strain a hit, and the barricade learns what that means when it takes Brand out of the fight through a splintered wagon — Brand [accepts a scar](core/strain-and-conditions.md#pushing-through--scars) (*Hears the Boiler in His Sleep*) and stays down a beat anyway.

<div class="callout example" data-title="Leverage across scale tiers">
<p><span class="speaker">Cass:</span> "I can't cut a steam-walker down with a saber — I know the <a href='#/combat/faction?id=scale'>scale rule</a>. So the goal is leverage: I go up its boarding ladder while it's mid-stride. I want its stoker hatch open and me inside it."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "That's the leverage the rule wants. Win this and your <em>next</em> goal can mark its track for real." <em>Physical 4, +1 from Rafe's timing, vs the walker's 4: 18–12.</em> "You're in the crew cage, saber against shovel-men."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Odile (Heart, Kindler):</span> "The line watched Brand fall. Before fear sets in — <strong>Marshal</strong>: I clear the militia's <em>Wavering</em> with the rally, riding Marta's soup." <em>Conviction 4 +1 banked edge = 5 dice against the Ministry's morale pressure at 3: 19–8.</em> "They sing. Of course they sing."</p>
</div>

## Dawn

By beat four, *Hold Until Dawn* fills at 6/6 with *Break the Junction* stuck at 4: the Ministry withdraws behind the Ironshod — which limps, *Boiler Breached*, with Cass riding its roof like a parade float. The GM ends the conflict the way the [rules say to](combat/overview.md#when-to-enter-conflict): the moment the Ministry's will breaks, not when its tracks empty.

At the next faction turn the Junction becomes the Movement's newest **node** — born from an Expand clock the table had been ticking for three sessions — and seven players argue joyfully over which pools its yields should feed.

<div class="callout" data-title="What to notice">
<p>Seven players, four beats, one GM — under two hours. The duplicate Hands never stepped on each other (one held the line, one broke things, and their synergy stacked +1 Force at the next Yield). The resource moves — Commit, Channel, Deploy, Marshal — gave the non-fighters a full battle to play without a single attack roll among them. And the fight's best moment came from the scale rule saying <em>no</em> to a saber and <em>yes</em> to a boarding ladder.</p>
</div>
