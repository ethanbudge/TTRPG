# The Marlowe Vault

A five-player table running **the Big Job** ([genre frame](gm/genre-frames.md#the-big-job--a-modern-crew-assembles-a-heist)) — a modern crew whose faction is a garage, a fence, and a reputation. Tonight: the vault of Marlowe & Associates, private bank to people who don't use banks. This example shows a full [venture](core/overview.md#the-loop) — the brief, the job, and the ninety seconds where it all goes wrong.

*At the table: the GM · Dana as **Rook** (Head, Strategist) · Leo as **Tam** (Eye, Shadow) · Grace as **Wires** (Mind, Operator — chosen system: networks) · Sofia as **Delle** (Voice, Idol) · Ben as **Coyle** (Spine, Fixer).*

## The Brief

No planning phase drags in The Party — the crew states the shape of the job, [banks what preparation they can](faction/faction-turn.md#5--downtime), and rolls into the fiction. Coyle stakes 2 stock as a **War Chest** (his feature: mission budget anyone can spend mid-job). Rook's **War Council** hands Prepare edges to three of them. Delle has spent two weeks as the bank's favorite new client under a cover identity — established fiction, no roll yet.

## The Job, Until It Isn't

Tam ghosts in through the freight elevator (win). Wires loops the cameras from a bakery Wi-Fi across the street (win, *In the Zone* reroll unused). Delle keeps the night manager charmed in the lobby (win by 7 — boon: the manager hands her his keycard *voluntarily*, "just for a moment"). Then the vault.

<div class="callout example" data-title="The tie that turns the night">
<p><span class="speaker">Grace (Wires):</span> "Goal — I want the vault's time-lock convinced it's 6 a.m. Friday." <em>Mental 4, +1 Prepare edge, +1 War Chest stock (Coyle's budget buys the signal cloner), +1 more stock for the stolen maintenance codes: that's 4 + 3 — pool caps at 5, and the leftover edge overflows, stripping the vault's Formidable 5 down to 4.</em> "Rolling: 18."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Vault rolls… 18. Tie. You're acting: the lock chirps, believes you, and swings. Costly success — as the bolts retract, the sound wakes the one thing your recon never found: a silent seismic sensor under the floor plate. Somewhere across town, a phone is ringing. You have minutes, not hours. We're in <a href='#/combat/overview'>conflict</a> now — clockspeed Blitz. I'm starting two clocks: <em>The Take (4)</em> and <em>Response Arrives (4)</em>."</p>
</div>

## Ninety Seconds, Five Goals

<div class="callout example" data-title="One beat around the whole table">
<p><span class="speaker">Dana (Rook):</span> "<em>Contingency.</em> We planned for this — revealing it now: the getaway isn't the van. It's the armored courier truck Marlowe's expects at dawn, which Coyle's guy delivered early. It's parked in their own loading dock." <em>No roll — the Strategist's signature just rewrites the exit.</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">Leo (Tam):</span> "Goal: the take, fast — I want the Aldana deposit boxes emptied before the clock beats us." <em>Physical 4 vs 3: 16–9, strong win.</em> "Tick The Take twice."</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Sofia (Delle):</span> "I'm still in the lobby with a terrified night manager. Goal: he remembers me as the woman who <em>saved</em> him tonight. I want a witness who lies for us." <em>Emotional 4, +1 Position edge — he already trusts her; he handed her his keycard an hour ago: 5 dice vs his 2 — 19 to 7.</em></p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "He will tell the police a story with no Delle in it, forever. Response clock ticks anyway — sirens, distant. Wires, the dock cameras?"</p>
<p><span class="speaker">Grace:</span> "Goal: the courier truck leaves this dock invisible — every camera on the block sees an empty bay." <em>Rolls 4 dice against 3: 11–13.</em> <strong>Loss.</strong></p>
<p><span class="speaker">GM:</span> "Stakes land: the truck gets out — but one traffic camera two blocks east catches the plate, and you know it. That's not tonight's problem. That's a problem with a <em>clock on it</em>: 'The Plate Comes Back (6),' ticking between sessions. Faction-level heat."</p>
</div>

## What the Venture Fed the Faction

The take converts to 3 stock at the next [faction turn](faction/faction-turn.md) (the GM rules: 2 *Reputation in the Life*, 1 *Safehouse Funds*) — but *The Plate Comes Back* now sits on the faction sheet next to the Albanian outfit's clocks. At session's end, [perks](characters/advancement.md) go around: everyone +1 base, +1 agenda (the score funds the cause), and Ben takes his flaw perk — Coyle's *Greedy* made him lift a watch from the vault, off-manifest, and the table saw him do it.

<div class="callout" data-title="What to notice">
<p>Nothing in the heist used heist-specific rules. Preparation was perks and features; the alarm was a costly success; the escape was dueling clocks; the loose end became faction-level fiction with a clock on it. The genre lives in the table's language — the engine underneath never changed.</p>
</div>
