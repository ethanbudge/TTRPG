# Faction Conflict

A faction conflict is the same engine with the organization's body on the field. Everything from [Conflict Overview](combat/overview.md) still applies — beats, goals, contests, bands, clocks. What changes is **what you can reach for on your turn**: in a faction conflict, characters direct faction resources in addition to their own.

A conflict *becomes* a faction conflict the moment faction assets, stock, or scale actors enter it — whether that was the plan (a fleet engagement) or an escalation (the duel that lights the riot). Zooming in and out mid-conflict is normal and carries all strain, conditions, and clocks with it.

## The Four Resource Moves

On your beat in a faction conflict, your goal may be powered by any of these — they're additions to your options, never replacements:

### Channel

Act *through* the faction: roll a **faction stat** or a deployed **asset's rating** instead of your own stat, narrating how you direct it — the fleet screens the flank, the street preachers flood the square, the ledger-men freeze the rival's credit. Your class features still apply (a Voice channeling the propaganda corps adds their edges). Any PC can channel anything the fiction gives them authority over; contested authority is a scene, not a rule.

When a channeled action loses a contest, the harm lands on the **asset's track** or as faction-scale conditions — the org absorbs what the org attempted.

### Commit

Spend external **stock** for [Resource edges](core/contests.md#edges-and-setbacks) — 1 stock = 1 edge, max 2 per contest, type must fit the approach, and the stock must be *reachable*: on site, or at a node connected by an unbroken route. Logistics decide battles before dice do; this rule is why the Spine wins wars.

### Deploy

Bring an asset from a connected node into the engagement. This is your goal for the beat (the reserves arrive, the gunboat rounds the point). Deployed assets act on subsequent beats — channeled by a PC, or autonomously with their own rating.

### Marshal

Tend the machine under fire: your goal clears a condition from a unit or asset, rallies a *Routed* formation, or moves an asset one band. The Heart and Head shine here.

## Scale

Faction conflicts mix actors of different sizes. The Party uses three **scale tiers**:

| Tier | Actors |
| --- | --- |
| **Personal** | people, small teams |
| **Company** | squads, gangs, vessels, war machines — most assets |
| **Host** | armies, fleets, mass movements, whole nodes |

One rule governs crossings, in both directions:

> **Against a larger tier, force alone can't mark their track — you need leverage.** Your wins instead grant edges, tick clocks, create conditions, or exploit what the fiction offers. With genuine leverage — the magazine, the flagship's helm, the beloved general alone on the balcony — you mark strain normally.
>
> **Against a smaller tier, your wins mark 2 strain — but the small are hard to pin:** smaller-tier actors gain an edge to evade, hide from, or slip through larger-tier attention.

So the lone Hand on the flagship's bridge can't cut down a fleet with a sword — but killing the admiral (leverage) pins *Headless* on the whole formation. And the fleet can vaporize the Hand's position — if it can find one person in the smoke.

This rule is what makes mixed-scale scenes sing: the fleet battle overhead sets conditions on the boarding fight below; the boarding fight decides who commands the fleet.

## Worked Example — The Anvil of Callis Reach

*Spacefaring frame. The players' faction — the free-trader compact **Longhaul** — must break a blockade of Callis Station by the Hegemony picket. Clockspeed: Operation (beats are tens of minutes). Arena bands: the station (Contact), gun range (Close), the system approaches (Far). Objective clock: **Corridor Open (6)** — this is a breakout, not a war of annihilation.*

*On the field: Longhaul's assets — the armed freighter **Vindicta** (Physical 3, track 3) and a Zeal-drunk militia of dockhands (Conviction 3, track 3, Sweeping) — plus the four PCs. Hegemony: the cruiser **Anvil** (Host tier, Physical 4, track 5, protection 1, Long) and two corvettes (Company tier, pool 3, track 3 each).*

**Beat 1.**
*Yara (Head — Strategist)* channels the *Vindicta*: "Goal — draw both corvettes out of formation toward the refinery debris." Pool 3, +1 Commit (1 Intelligence stock — she knows their patrol doctrine), +1 her *Read the Board* answer = 5 vs corvettes' 3. **Win.** Corvettes take *Out of Position* (scene condition); ticks **Corridor Open → 1/6**.

*Dez (Hand — Breaker)*, already aboard the *Anvil* from an earlier infiltration — Personal tier against a Host: no track marks without leverage. "Goal — I'm *making* leverage: charges on the primary magazine." Physical 4 vs difficulty 4 (hardened compartment). **Tie — costly success.** Charges set (leverage established!), but the cost: boarding alarms — Dez is *Hunted* aboard.

*GM (the Anvil acts):* Host-tier guns rake the militia barges (Company tier — one tier down: 2 strain on a win). Anvil 4+1 (Long, uncontested range) = 5 vs militia 3. **Win by 5 — strong.** The militia marks 2, takes *Routed*. Bad beat. The dockhands break.

**Beat 2.**
*Sister Ovel (Heart — Kindler)* channels nothing — she opens an all-ships channel. **Fire Rises:** the *Routed* condition on the militia ends instantly, no roll. Then her goal — rally them back into the fight: Conviction 4 +1 (*Rousing Word* deepened) = 5 vs difficulty 3. **Win.** Militia re-forms at Close; **Corridor Open → 2/6**.

*Dez:* "Goal — blow the magazine and get to the pods." With leverage established, this can mark the Host. Physical 4 vs 4 (*Hunted* setback → 3 vs 4). **Loses.** Stakes land: captured at the charges — the detonator in Hegemony hands, Dez in a cell. (The table winces. The Eye starts planning.)

*Yara,* seeing it unravel, **Commits 2 Force stock** (the reserve squadron held at Callis — route intact): channels the whole compact's strength, pool 3+2 = 5 vs Anvil's 4: "Goal — mass every gun on the Anvil's launch bays so no pursuit can follow the convoy." **Win by 6 — strong.** Anvil takes 1 strain (Company-vs-Host: the massed squadron *is* the leverage), plus boon: *Launch Bays Burning* condition. **Corridor Open → 4/6.**

**Beat 3.**
The corvettes, *Out of Position*, race back — GM rules they can't reach Close this beat (two-band move is their whole goal). The convoy runs the corridor: one last channeled contest, freighters' 3 +1 (bays burning) +1 (militia screen) = 5 vs Anvil's 4 −1 (strain condition) = 3. **Win.** **Corridor Open → 6/6.** The blockade is broken.

*The conflict ends by objective. The Anvil still floats — barely scratched, twice humiliated. Dez is her prisoner, which is next session. Total table time: three beats, nine rolls, one fleet action.*

## Wars That Take Months

Stretch the clockspeed to **Season** or **Era** and the same example becomes a trade war: beats are weeks, goals are *"we dump subsidized grain until their carriers sail empty,"* Commit burns Influence instead of Force, and the objective clock reads *Their Charter Revoked (8)*. Between beats, [faction turns](faction/faction-turn.md) keep running — long wars are fought in the logistics step as much as the conflict scenes. When a single week of the war turns hot, zoom in, fight it at Blitz, and zoom back out with the consequences.
