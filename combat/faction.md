# Faction Conflict

A faction conflict is the same engine with the organization's body on the field. Everything from [Conflict Overview](combat/overview.md) applies — exchanges, goals, contests, bands, countdowns. What changes is what you can reach for on your turn: in a faction conflict, characters direct faction resources in addition to their own.

A conflict *becomes* a faction conflict the moment faction assets, stock, or larger-scale actors enter it — whether that was the plan (a fleet engagement) or an escalation (the duel that lights the riot). Zooming in and out mid-conflict is normal and carries all strain, conditions, and countdowns along.

## The Four Resource Moves

On your exchange in a faction conflict, your goal may be powered by any of these. They add options; they never replace your own:

### Channel

Act through the faction: roll a **faction stat** or a deployed **asset's rating** instead of your own stat, narrating how you direct it — the fleet screens the flank, the street preachers flood the square, the ledger-men freeze the rival's credit. Your Seat features still apply. Any character can channel anything the narrative gives them authority over; contested authority is a scene, not a rule.

When a channeled action loses, the harm lands on the asset's strain or as faction-scale conditions — the organization absorbs what the organization attempted.

### Commit

Spend external stock for a [Faction Resource edge](core/contests.md#edges-and-snags) — 1 stock, 1 edge, the type must fit the approach, and the stock must be **[Ready](faction/nodes.md#accessibility)**. Where you stashed things last month decides what you can commit tonight; this rule is why the Spine wins wars.

### Deploy

Bring a Reachable asset into the engagement. This is your whole goal for the exchange — the reserves arrive, the gunboat rounds the point. Deployed assets act on later exchanges, channeled by a character or autonomously on their own rating.

### Marshal

Tend the machine under fire: your goal clears a condition from a unit or asset, rallies a *Routed* formation, or moves an asset one band. The Heart and Head shine here.

## Scale

Faction conflicts mix actors of different sizes. The Party uses three tiers:

| Tier | Actors |
| --- | --- |
| **Personal** | people, small teams |
| **Company** | squads, gangs, vessels, war machines — most assets |
| **Host** | armies, fleets, mass movements |

One rule governs crossings, both directions:

> **Against a larger tier, force alone can't mark their strain — you need leverage.** Your wins instead grant edges, strike countdowns, create conditions, or exploit what the situation offers. With genuine leverage, you mark strain normally.
>
> **The leverage test:** leverage is a named vulnerability established in the narrative *before* the harmful goal is declared — the magazine Dez rigged, the flagship's helm, the beloved general alone on the balcony. Mass is never leverage; if enough force gathers to genuinely threaten the larger tier, treat the massed actor as one tier up and skip the argument.
>
> **Against a smaller tier, your wins mark 2 strain — but the small are hard to pin:** smaller actors gain an edge to evade, hide from, or slip through larger attention.

The lone Fist on the flagship's bridge can't cut down a fleet with a sword — but killing the admiral (leverage) pins *Headless* on the whole formation. And the fleet can vaporize the Fist's position, if it can find one person in the smoke.

## Worked Example — The Anvil of Callis Reach

*Spacefaring frame. The players' faction — the free-trader compact **Longhaul** — must break the Hegemony picket blockading Callis Station. Clockspeed: Operation (exchanges are tens of minutes). Bands: the station (Contact), gun range (Close), the approaches (Far). Objective countdown: **Corridor Open (6)** — a breakout, not a war of annihilation.*

*On the field: Longhaul's assets — the armed freighter **Vindicta** (Physical 3, strain 3) and a zeal-drunk militia of dockhands (Ideological 3, strain 3, Sweeping) — plus the four characters. Hegemony: the cruiser **Anvil** (Host tier, Physical 4, strain 5, protection 1, Long) and two corvettes (Company tier, pool 3, strain 3 each).*

**Exchange 1.**
*Yara (Head — Strategist)* channels the *Vindicta*: "Goal — draw both corvettes out of formation toward the refinery debris." Pool 3, +1 Commit (1 Intelligence stock, Ready — their patrol doctrine, bought dear last season), +1 Position (she chose this debris field) = 5 vs. the corvettes' 3. **Win.** Corvettes take *Out of Position*; **Corridor Open: 5 left.**

*Dez (Fist — Breaker)*, already aboard the *Anvil* from an earlier infiltration — Personal against Host, no strain without leverage. "Goal — I'm *making* leverage: charges on the primary magazine." Physical 4 vs. difficulty 4. **Tie — costly success.** Charges set, leverage established; the cost: boarding alarms. Dez is *Hunted* aboard.

*GM (the Anvil acts):* Host guns rake the militia barges — one tier down, 2 strain on a win. Anvil 4 +1 (Long, uncontested range) = 5 vs. militia 3. **Rolls 21 vs 9 — a Triumph.** The militia marks 2, and the boon pins *Routed*. The dockhands break.

**Exchange 2.**
*Sister Ovel (Heart — Kindler)* opens an all-ships channel. **Fire Rises:** she names the militia — the *Routed* clears if they turn back toward the corridor now, and the turn comes with an edge. Their re-forming surge: Ideological 3 +1 = 4 vs. difficulty 3. **Win.** They sing their way back to Close. **Corridor Open: 4 left.**

*Dez:* "Goal — blow the magazine and get to the pods." Leverage established, so this can mark the Host. Physical 4, *Hunted* (snag → 3) vs. 4. **Loss.** The stakes land: taken at the charges, the detonator in Hegemony hands, Dez in a cell. The table winces. The Eye starts planning.

*Yara,* watching it unravel: "Goal — mass every gun we have on the Anvil's launch bays, so nothing can pursue the convoy." Channels the compact: pool 3, +1 Commit (1 Force stock — the reserve squadron, held Ready at Callis on her order two turns ago), +1 Aid (the *Vindicta* screens the run) = 5 vs. the Anvil's 4. **Rolls 22 vs 13 — a Triumph.** Freighter guns can't gut a cruiser — Company against Host, and mass is never leverage — so the win does what up-tier wins do: the goal itself. The Anvil takes *Launch Bays Burning*, and the boon strikes the objective. **Corridor Open: 2 left.**

**Exchange 3.**
The corvettes race back — *Out of Position*, the GM rules they can't reach Close this exchange; the two-band move is their whole goal. The convoy runs the corridor: freighters 3, +1 Position (no pursuit can launch), +1 Aid (militia screen) = 5 vs. the Anvil's 4, snagged by its burning bays = 3. **Rolls 17 vs 9 — a Triumph. Corridor Open: done.** The blockade is broken.

*The conflict ends by objective. The Anvil still floats — unscratched, twice humiliated, wearing a condition it will remember. Dez is her prisoner, which is next session. Three exchanges, nine rolls, one fleet action.*

## Wars That Take Months

Stretch the clockspeed to Season or Era and the same example becomes a trade war: exchanges are weeks, goals are *"we dump subsidized grain until their carriers sail empty,"* Commit burns Influence instead of Force, and the objective reads *Their Charter Revoked (8)*. Between exchanges, [faction turns](faction/faction-turn.md) keep running — long wars are won in the Keep and on the map as much as in the battles. When a single week turns hot, zoom in, fight it at Blitz, and zoom back out with the consequences.
