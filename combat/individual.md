# Personal Conflict

Personal conflict is the [conflict engine](combat/overview.md) at human scale: your own four stats, your own strain track, your own kit. It covers everything from swordplay to shouting matches — a conflict is any struggle of wills, and *strain* is whatever gets worn down: flesh in a fight, composure in an interrogation, standing in a court intrigue.

## The Shape of a Fight

1. GM sets the **clockspeed** (usually Blitz), sketches the arena in **bands**, and names the opposition out loud — pools, tracks, and any visible tags. The Party doesn't hide the enemy's dice; tension comes from odds, not ignorance.
2. Each beat: every actor declares one **goal** and resolves one **contest**. Harm on a lost contest = 1 strain (2 on a margin of 5+). [Protection](core/strain-and-conditions.md#protection) absorbs; [conditions](core/strain-and-conditions.md#conditions) change what's possible.
3. The fight ends when a side's will is broken — taken out, routed, objective settled, or terms offered. Most fights should end in **3–5 beats**. If it's dragging, the GM escalates stakes or offers terms; grinding tracks is never the intended experience.

## Opposition Templates

| Threat | Pool | Track | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mook / obstacle | 2 | 1–2 | Often acts via stakes, not goals; *Sweeping* goals clear groups |
| Professional | 3 | 3 | One tag or trick |
| Lieutenant | 4 | 4 | A gift-equivalent and a protection tag |
| Nemesis | 4–5 | 5 | Features like a PC; earns their name |

## Worked Example — The Grinning Lantern Goes Loud

*Fantasy heist frame. Sable (**Eye**, Physical 3 / Mental 4) and Brakk (**Hand — Warden**, Physical 4) are extracting a defecting clerk from a tavern when four Consortium tariff men (mooks, pool 2, track 1) and their captain, Ossler (lieutenant, pool 4, track 4, protection 1), block the door. Clockspeed: Blitz. Arena: taproom (Contact–Close).*

**Beat 1.**
*Sable:* "Goal — I get the clerk to the cellar stairs before anyone can grab her." Physical 3, +1 edge (*Small*, weaving the crowd) = 4 dice vs. mooks' 2. **Rolls 15 vs 7 — strong win.** The clerk reaches the stairs; boon: Sable banks an edge for Brakk ("I shoulder a table over as cover — go!").

*Brakk:* "Goal — I put myself between Ossler and that stairwell, and I want him hesitant." Physical 4 + banked edge = 5 dice vs. Ossler's 4. **Rolls 19 vs 16 — win.** Brakk fills the doorway; Ossler takes *Hesitant* (scene condition, setback to advance on Brakk).

*GM (Ossler's goal):* "He waves the tariff men at you in a rush — they want you buried under bodies." Mooks swarm: pool 2 +1 edge (numbers) = 3 vs. Brakk's 4. **Rolls 11 vs 11 — tie.** Ossler initiated, so the swarm's goal lands *at a cost*: Brakk marks 1 strain (absorbed — protection tag, shield up, 0 taken) but he's *Pinned* (hard condition: can't change bands; exit named: break free with Physical, or an ally frees him).

**Beat 2.**
*Sable:* "Goal — from the stairs I put a knife through the lantern rope; darkness in the taproom." Physical 3 vs. difficulty 3. **Rolls 12 vs 8 — win.** The room drops into dark. GM: mooks are *Blinded*; Sable and Brakk fight in the dark they chose (Sable has *Night Eyes* — no setback).

*Brakk (Interpose already armed):* "Goal — I break the pin and carry the nearest tariff man through the cellar door with me." Physical 4 vs. mooks' 2, mooks *Blinded* (setback → pool 1). **Rolls 17 vs 3 — strong win.** Pin broken, mook taken out (track 1), boon: door barred behind them.

*Ossler,* Hesitant, blinded, his men tangled in the dark, **offers terms** through the door: walk away tonight, and no report names them. The fight is over in two beats — not because the tracks emptied, but because his will did.

*(Note what never happened: no initiative order, no attack-vs-AC, no damage roll, no condition lookup. Four goals, four contests, one tie doing story work, and a fight that ended by decision.)*

## Duels of Every Kind

The same structure runs non-violent showdowns — swap the fiction of strain:

- **Interrogation:** strain = composure. Taken out = you break, or shut down entirely.
- **Court intrigue:** strain = standing. Conditions like *Discredited* and *Out of Favor* do the maiming.
- **A chase:** run it as dueling clocks (*Escape 4* vs. *Cornered 4*) with bands doing the geography.

If it can be lost by inches, it can be a conflict.
