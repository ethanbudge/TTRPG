# Personal Conflict

Personal conflict is the [conflict engine](combat/overview.md) at human scale: your own stats, your own strain boxes, your own kit. It covers everything from swordplay to shouting matches — a conflict is any struggle of wills, and strain is whatever gets worn down: flesh in a fight, composure in an interrogation, standing in a court intrigue.

## The Shape of a Fight

1. The GM sets the clockspeed (usually Blitz), sketches the arena in bands, and names the opposition out loud — pools, strain, visible tags, and whether this conflict can kill. The Party doesn't hide the enemy's dice; tension comes from odds, not ignorance.
2. Each exchange: every actor declares one goal and resolves one contest — the players *and* every enemy that can act, each pressing their own goal. Harm on a lost contest is 1 strain, 2 on a Disaster. [Protection](core/strain-and-conditions.md#protection) absorbs; [conditions](core/strain-and-conditions.md#conditions) change what's possible.
3. The fight ends when a side's will breaks — broken actors, a settled objective, terms offered. Most fights end inside three to five exchanges. If it's dragging, the GM escalates stakes or offers terms.

## Opposition Templates

| Threat | Pool | Strain | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mook / obstacle | 2 | 1–2 | A crowd acts as one goal or via stakes; *Sweeping* goals clear groups |
| Professional | 3 | 3 | One tag or trick; takes its own goal each exchange |
| Lieutenant | 4 | 4 | A gift-equivalent and a protection tag |
| Nemesis | 4–5 | 4–5 | Features like a PC; earns their name |

## Worked Example — The Grinning Lantern Goes Loud

*Fantasy frame. Sable (**Eye**, Physical 3 / Mental 4) and Brakk (**Fist — Warden**, Physical 4) are extracting a defecting clerk from a tavern when four Consortium tariff men (mooks, pool 2, strain 1) and their captain, Ossler (lieutenant, pool 4, strain 4, protection 1), block the door. Clockspeed: Blitz. Arena: taproom, Contact to Close. The GM notes the stakes plainly: steel is out — this one can kill.*

**Exchange 1.**
*Sable:* "Goal — I get the clerk to the cellar stairs before anyone can grab her." Physical 3, +1 edge (*Small*, weaving the crowd) = 4 dice vs. the mooks' 2. **Rolls 16 vs 7 — a Triumph.** The clerk reaches the stairs, and Sable spends the boon priming an edge for Brakk ("I shoulder a table over as cover — go!").

*Brakk:* "Goal — I put myself between Ossler and that stairwell, and I want him hesitant about it." Physical 4 + primed edge = 5 dice vs. Ossler's 4. **Rolls 19 vs 16 — win.** Brakk fills the doorway; Ossler takes *Hesitant* (conflict condition — snag on pressing the attack).

*GM (Ossler's goal):* "He waves the tariff men into a rush — they want you buried under bodies." The swarm: pool 2 +1 edge (numbers) = 3 vs. Brakk's 4. **Rolls 11 vs 11 — tie.** The swarm initiated, so its goal lands with full teeth: Brakk marks 1 strain (negated — protection tag, shield up) and he's *Pinned* (hard condition; exit named: break free with Physical, or an ally frees him). And the cost bills the acting side: the crush leaves the nearest tariff man sprawled across Brakk's shield — a Position edge on Brakk's next contest against them.

**Exchange 2.**
*Sable:* "Goal — from the stairs I put a knife through the lantern rope. Darkness." Physical 3 vs. difficulty 3. **Rolls 12 vs 8 — win.** The room drops into black. The mooks are *Blinded*; Sable has *Night Eyes* and fights in the dark she chose.

*Brakk:* "Goal — I break the pin and carry the nearest tariff man through the cellar door with me." Physical 4 + Position edge = 5 vs. the mooks' 2, *Blinded* (snag → 1). **Rolls 18 vs 4 — a Triumph.** Pin broken, one mook broken with it; the boon bars the door behind them.

*Ossler* — hesitant, blind, his men tangled in the dark — offers terms through the door: walk away tonight, and no report names anyone. The fight is over in two exchanges. Not because anyone's boxes ran out; because his will did.

## Duels of Every Kind

The same structure runs non-violent showdowns — swap what strain means:

- **Interrogation:** strain is composure. Broken means you talk, or shut down entirely.
- **Court intrigue:** strain is standing. Conditions like *Discredited* and *Out of Favor* do the maiming.
- **A chase:** run it as dueling countdowns (*Escape (4)* vs. *Cornered (4)*) with bands doing the geography.

If it can be lost by inches, it can be a conflict.
