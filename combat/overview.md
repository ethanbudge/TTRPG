# Conflict Overview

The Party has one conflict system. A cellar knife-fight, a shouted parliamentary ambush, a three-month trade war, and a fleet action in a debris field all run on the same procedure — **exchanges, goals, contests, strain** — at different zoom levels. [Personal Conflict](combat/individual.md) and [Faction Conflict](combat/faction.md) add scale-specific texture; this page is the engine they share.

## When to Enter Conflict

Enter structured conflict when more than one actor urgently wants incompatible things and the order of events matters. Otherwise stay in free scene play with ordinary contests. Leaving conflict matters just as much: the moment one side's will is broken — strain filled, conditions decisive, objective settled — stop counting exchanges and return to the conversation.

## Timescale

At the start of a conflict the GM declares what one exchange represents:

| Clockspeed | One exchange is… | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Blitz** | seconds | brawls, firefights, boarding actions |
| **Operation** | minutes–hours | heists, manhunts, sieges-in-progress |
| **Season** | days–weeks | trade wars, election campaigns, blockades |
| **Era** | months+ | generational struggles, cold wars |

Nothing else changes. A goal at Blitz is *"I drag the captain behind the barricade"*; at Season it's *"I flood their harbor with cheap grain until their merchants defect."* Same dice, same outcomes.

## Exchanges & the Spotlight

Each exchange, every actor gets **one goal** — each PC, each significant NPC or rival asset. There is no initiative count:

- The table picks the order from the situation — who's moving *now*? When in doubt, go around the table, players first.
- The GM's significant actors take their goals woven between player goals. Mooks and hazards mostly don't take goals at all — they act through **stakes**, arriving as the cost of player losses and ties.
- A goal is one sentence. If a turn is taking more than a couple of minutes, the GM asks for the sentence, sets the dice, and rolls.

**Goals, not moves.** Declare the outcome you want (*"I want their flank commander dead or fleeing"*), never a mechanic (*"I attack twice"*). The table maps the sentence to a stat, edges, and stakes. Anything a person could try, a goal can attempt — disarm, humiliate, stampede the cattle, hold the door, buy time, change the subject of the war.

<div class="callout" data-title="Defending is free">
<p>Rolling to oppose someone else's goal never consumes your own — you always get your exchange. A feature costs you your goal only if its text says so; features that trigger "when" something happens ride alongside your turn, not instead of it.</p>
</div>

## Distance — The Four Bands

Space is measured in four **range bands**, sized relative to the arena:

| Band | Knife fight | City operation | Fleet action | Trade war |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Contact** | grappling | same room | boarding / docked | same market, face to face |
| **Close** | same room | same block | gun range / same orbit | same city |
| **Far** | across the hall | across the district | same system, hours away | same region |
| **Distant** | out of the fight | across the city | other systems | across the world |

- Moving one band rides along with your goal if nothing opposes it; if something does, the movement is the contest.
- Moving two or more bands is your whole goal for the exchange.
- Tools, weapons, and assets carry **range tags** — a blade works at Contact, a rifle at Close/Far, a broadside at Close, a propaganda press at Distant. Range tags are permissions, not bonuses: they define which goals you can declare from where.

## Tags

A short, closed list of descriptive tags covers what other games do with equipment tables:

| Tag | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Piercing** | Ignores protection tags |
| **Sweeping** | The goal may target a cluster — a squad, a crowd, a formation |
| **Silent** | Wins don't raise alarms |
| **Anchored** | Can't change bands (emplacements, fortresses, institutions) |
| **Long** | Only usable at Far or Distant (artillery, torpedoes, market manipulation) |
| **Protection** | Negate 1 incoming strain, once per conflict ([details](core/strain-and-conditions.md#protection)) |

That's the entire equipment system.

## Build-Up & Payoff

How fast turns still produce crescendos:

- **Set-up goals.** Any win can **prime an edge for a named ally** instead of taking effect, if the story connects them ("I pin them down — go!"). A character can hold one primed edge at a time — it's the [Primed source](core/contests.md#edges-and-snags), cap 1 — and primed edges expire when the conflict ends.
- **Triumphs** (win by 8+) hand out boons — extra strain, positions, conditions, countdown segments.
- **Conditions are combos.** *Blinded* on the duelist, then the disarm. *Discredited* on the minister, then the vote. The payoff for teamwork isn't bigger numbers — it's changing what the situation *is* before the decisive roll.
- **Countdowns in conflict.** Objectives beyond "drop them" get countdowns: *Overload the Reactor (4)*, *The Vault Opens (6)*, *Their Coalition Splits (8)*. Fights with countdowns in them end by objective, not annihilation — usually faster, always more interesting.

## Zooming

Conflicts change scale mid-stream, and the system follows without switching rules:

- **Zoom out** when faction resources commit or larger actors join — the duel spills into a riot, the boarding action becomes a fleet engagement. Faction pools, assets, and [resource moves](combat/faction.md) come online; the clockspeed may lengthen. Strain, conditions, positions, and countdowns carry over untouched.
- **Zoom in** when one thread of a big conflict becomes the story — freeze the fleet battle, play the bridge duel at Blitz, then zoom back out with its outcome applied as conditions, edges, or countdown segments.
- Who can hurt whom across scales is one rule: [Scale](combat/faction.md#scale).
