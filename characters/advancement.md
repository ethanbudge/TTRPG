# Perks & Advancement

There are no levels and no experience points in The Party. There is one currency — the **perk** — and one recurring question: *yourself, or the faction?*

Do you spend the evening practicing your swordwork, or drilling the recruits? Study the ancient codex, or catalog it for the archive? Every perk asks it again.

## Earning Perks

**In the moment:**

- **+1** when one of your [flaws](characters/traits.md) materially complicates your situation (max 1 per flaw per session).
- **+1** when you accept a [scar](core/strain-and-conditions.md#pushing-through--scars).

**At session's end,** ask the table (max 3):

- **+1** — you showed up and played your office. (Everyone gets this.)
- **+1** — the faction's [agenda](faction/overview.md#the-agenda) advanced this session, in part because of you.
- **+1** — you pursued a personal ambition — something *yours*, declared aloud at a previous session's end or at creation.

A typical session yields **2–3 perks**. Perks bank without limit.

## Spending Perks

### In play — 1 perk

Once per contest: **reroll** one of your dice after seeing the result, or **Prepare** — declare pre-roll groundwork your character plausibly laid earlier, gaining a Position edge.

### On yourself — personal advances

| Advance | Cost |
| --- | --- |
| New feature from your class list | **4** |
| New feature from another class's list (fiction must support the cross-training) | **5** |
| New gift | **4** |
| +1 to a stat (max 5) | **8** |

### On the faction — faction advances

| Advance | Cost |
| --- | --- |
| New [asset](faction/nodes.md#assets) — you trained it, built it, or recruited it personally | **3** |
| Node upgrade (fortification, warehouse, workshop…) | **3** |
| New route between nodes | **2** |
| Recruit a named NPC specialist for the faction | **4** |
| Found a new node — your personal project becomes a chapter | **5** |
| +1 to a faction stat (max 5) — **players may pool perks** | **8** |

Faction advances are deliberately **cheaper** than personal ones. Organizational investment should feel like the sensible choice you keep almost making — that tension is the game.

Note the two economies stay separate everywhere else: perks never convert directly into [stock](faction/resources.md), and stock never buys advances. Your time and your faction's resources are different things; the perk table is the *only* bridge, and it's a one-way street called effort.

## Deepening

Every **third advance you buy** (personal or faction — what matters is that *you* funded it), **Deepen** one feature you own, free. Choose one template:

- **Again** — one extra use per scene/session of a limited feature.
- **Wider** — the feature affects one more target, one more range band, or a group instead of a person.
- **Sharper** — the feature's edge becomes two edges, or its effect upgrades one step (1 strain cleared → 2; condition downgraded → removed).

The GM approves combinations; a feature can be Deepened twice, with different templates. Deepening is this draft's answer to deep subclass trees without writing sixty more features — see [Open Questions](appendix/open-questions.md) for whether full calling tiers should replace it.

## Power From Session One

The math holds advancement honest: a starting character with a 4-stat wins even contests three times out of four *on day one*. Advances never raise those odds much — the stat cap is 5, edges cap at the pool clamp, and strain tracks never grow (Hand excepted). What advancement buys is **breadth**: more features, more once-per-session tricks, more of the faction's machinery answering to you. A veteran of thirty sessions isn't harder to kill than a rookie — they're *impossible to corner*, because they have an answer for everything.

This is deliberate, and it's the core of the brief this game was designed from: **leveling up means doing additional cool things, not finally being able to do cool things.**
