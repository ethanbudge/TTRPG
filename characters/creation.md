# Character Creation

A character in The Party is defined by their **role in the faction** first and everything else second. Creation takes about 20 minutes and produces a character who is already genuinely good at their job — advancement widens what you can do, it doesn't rescue you from incompetence.

Build the faction together *before or alongside* characters — see [Faction Overview](faction/overview.md). Characters are written into an organization, not bolted on afterward.

## The Seven Steps

### 1. Concept & Office

Answer in one sentence each:

- **Who are you?** (the fiction: ex-priest, getaway driver, ship's doctor, guild fence)
- **What is your office in the faction?** (the mechanics: which [class](characters/classes.md) — your organizational role, which may have nothing to do with your day job)

A ship's *cook* can be the faction's **Heart**. The *accountant* can be its **Eye**. The tension or harmony between day job and office is free characterization.

### 2. Class & Calling

Choose one of the seven classes — **Head, Voice, Hand, Eye, Mind, Heart, Spine** — and one of its three **callings** (subclasses). You get:

- your class's **core feature** (always on),
- your calling's **signature feature**,
- **two more features** chosen from your class's feature list.

Duplicated classes are fine and intended to work — two Hands are a formidable pair, two Voices a media empire. Missing classes are also fine: any player can perform any faction function; class holders just do it with style and mechanical teeth.

### 3. Stats

Assign one array across **Physical, Mental, Emotional, Conviction**:

- **Focused** 4/3/2/2 · **Balanced** 3/3/3/2 · **Spiky** 4/4/2/1

### 4. Gifts & Flaws

Pick **two gifts** and **one or two flaws** from [Gifts & Flaws](characters/traits.md). Flaws earn you perks when they complicate your life — take a second one if you want a stormier, faster-advancing character.

### 5. Bonds

Answer three questions out loud at the table:

1. **Why do you believe?** What made you give yourself to this faction's cause? (If the answer is "I don't — yet," even better.)
2. **What has the faction cost you?** Something is already gone: a home, a name, a person, a clean conscience.
3. **Who here would you trust with your life — and who not quite?** Point at PCs.

Bonds have no numbers. They exist so the GM can aim at them.

### 6. Kit

Write down a plausible loadout for your fiction and genre — gear in The Party is **fictional permission**, not a stat block. A sniper rifle doesn't add dice; it lets you declare goals at Far range that a knife-holder can't. Exceptions:

- Up to **two items** may carry a **protection tag** or a descriptive tag like *Piercing*, *Sweeping*, *Silent*, or a range tag (see [Conflict Overview](combat/overview.md#tags)) — mark these with the GM.
- Genuinely scarce or faction-owned equipment (the drop ship, the printing press, the reliquary) belongs on the faction sheet as an [asset](faction/nodes.md#assets), not in your pocket.

### 7. Track & Perks

- Strain track: **5 boxes**.
- Start with **1 perk**, banked.
