# The Heel

> *"Give me a fast horse and a day's head start and I'll lose an army. Give me two days and I'll bring it back around behind them."*
> — Wren Ashlock, courier of the Free Cantons

The Heel holds the Seat of **movement and reconnaissance**: the scout, ranger, outrider, pilot, navigator, or messenger. When the faction needs eyes on the far ridge, a package across three checkpoints, or a hand at the helm through the debris field, the Heel is already moving.

## Core Feature — The Fast Way

You always know a way through — a shortcut, a gap in the patrol, a route no map shows. Gain an edge on contests to travel, scout ahead, break contact, or carry word and goods through danger. Distance and difficult ground bend for you where they'd stop anyone else.

## Faction Synergy

**Put Down Roots** and actions to scout, expand, or move stock through danger gain an edge when a Heel helps run them. If the faction has more than one Heel, it also gains 1 extra **internal Ideological** stock (*Unity*) at [Income](faction/faction-turn.md#1--income) — the couriers keep the far-flung body feeling like one thing. ## Callings

### The Shadow — *goes where nothing goes*

Locked doors and sealed decks are suggestions.

**Signature — Ghost Protocol:** once per session, when a barrier or hard condition blocks your movement or perception — the lockdown, the tractor field, the sealed vault — roll Mental or Physical against 4. Win: you're past it, and you narrate how. Loss: it holds, and something notices the attempt (Ethan note: triumph and disaster stipulation).

### The Pilot — *one with the machine*

Choose your craft at creation: a ship, a flyer, a mount, a war-rig, a getaway car.

**Signature — Redline:** while you operate your chosen craft, gain an edge on contests of maneuver, pursuit, and evasion. Once per conflict, push it past what it should bear — reroll one of your dice; if that contest still loses, the craft takes 1 strain or a fitting condition.

### The Ranger — *reads the ground*

Tracker, pathfinder, hunter of the far country.

**Signature — Lay of the Land:** in any terrain you've had time to range through, you and your allies ignore terrain snags and cannot be ambushed.
