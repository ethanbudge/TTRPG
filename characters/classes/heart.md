# The Heart

> *"We lost the harvest, the chapel, and nineteen good people. We did not lose the choir. Ask me which one saved us."*
> — Mother Aveline, rebuilding year

The Heart holds the Seat of **morale and cohesion**: the chaplain, medic, counselor, bard, cook, or den mother. Every organization runs on something no ledger tracks. The Heart tends it — and in play, the Heart is the recovery engine and the reason the faction's people feel real.

## Core Feature — Keep the Flame

Once per conflict, tend to an ally — a word, a drink, a prayer, a look across the smoke. Clear 1 strain from them, or clear one morale or social condition (*Shaken*, *Routed*, *Demoralized*).

## Faction Synergy

**Quell** and **Tend** actions (calming unrest, healing the faction's own) gain an edge when a Heart helps run them. If the faction has more than one Heart, it also gains 1 extra internal Emotional stock at [Income](faction/faction-turn.md#1--income).

## Seat Features (start with two)

| Feature | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **We Eat Together** | Once per faction turn, meet one unpaid need from the faction's [Keep](faction/faction-turn.md#2--keep) — any type — with your own two hands. No stock is spent and no condition lands. Tell the table what you did. You can't cover the same need twice running. |
| **Confessor** | Edge on contests to read someone's true feelings or draw out what they're holding back. Once per session, after honest time with an NPC, roll Emotional against their pool. Win: they confide something true and useful. Loss: they share only what anyone could have told you. |
| **Get Up** | Once per session, when an ally is [broken](core/strain-and-conditions.md#broken), go to them and roll Ideological against 3. Win: they're not done — they rejoin the conflict with 1 box cleared, on their feet because you asked them to be. This can't undo a mortal exit already chosen. |
| **Binding Oath** | Officiate a pact between two characters. While both keep it, each gains an edge on contests directly serving the pact; whoever breaks it takes a lasting condition. |
| **Homefront** | Among the faction's own people, simple hospitality — a meal, a bed, a closed door — never needs a roll, and you gain an edge on contests to call on them for real help: the hiding place, the borrowed cart, the crowd that swears you were never there. |
| **Rousing Word** | Once per conflict, speak to what an ally believes before they roll: they gain an edge on a contest that serves the faction's cause. This isn't Aid — you share no stakes. You just believe in them louder than they doubt themselves. |

## Callings

### The Mender — *bodies*

Field surgeon, herbalist, med-bay chief.

**Signature — Miracle Worker:** when you have real time and quiet to treat someone, roll Mental or Ideological against 3. Win: clear all their strain and one lasting condition. Triumph: every condition too. Loss: clear 2 strain, and you learn exactly what you'd need to do better — the GM says what's missing.

### The Kindler — *fires*

You don't heal fear. You burn it as fuel.

**Signature — Fire Rises:** once per conflict, name an ally carrying a morale condition (*Shaken*, *Doubting*, *Routed* — a unit counts). If they immediately act toward the cause, the condition clears and that contest gains an edge. Fear, spent like powder.

### The Shepherd — *roots*

Communities, congregations, crews — you grow belonging where you stand.

**Signature — Roots:** wherever you've settled in and served — given real time among a place's people — they treat the faction as their own: hospitality without asking, a warning before trouble, and one protection tag for faction members there, refreshing each faction turn.
