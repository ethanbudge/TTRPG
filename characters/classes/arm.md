# The Arm

> *"They asked for a bridge by dawn. I gave them a bridge by dawn. Nobody asked what it was standing on, so I never said."*
> — Chief Wright Bel Osra, siege train of the Ninth

The Arm holds the Seat of **building and repair**: the engineer, artificer, wright, sapper, or shipwright. Whatever a setting calls its makers — the one who patches the hull mid-burn, raises the siege tower, keeps the reactor lit, or shapes a city out of raw stone and stranger materials — that is the Arm. The [Mind](characters/classes/mind.md) knows how the world works; the Arm *makes it work anyway*.

## Core Feature — Of Course I Can Build It

Once per session, name a feat of construction, repair, or artifice the table agrees is possible for your discipline but absurd under pressure — a working cannon from scrap, a breach sealed under fire, a wardstone raised before the moon sets, a drive rebuilt mid-chase. The GM must allow the goal to be attempted, and you roll it with an edge. Your craft doesn't make the impossible safe. It makes it *stand up long enough*.

## Faction Synergy

**Develop** and **Put Down Roots** actions that build, fortify, or repair gain an edge when an Arm helps run them. If the faction has more than one Arm, it also gains 1 extra **external Ideological** stock (*grand works — cathedrals, monuments, ramparts that make the faction's name mean something*) at [Income](faction/faction-turn.md#1--income).

## Seat Features (start with two)

| Feature | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Jury-Rig** | Once per conflict, build from what's at hand and choose the result: it opens one barred way (a door, a channel, a wound closed), grants a Personal Resource edge to one contest, or serves as a protection tag. Whatever it was, it dies spectacularly when the conflict ends. |
| **The Good Stuff** | Your personal kit is masterwork. Each session, choose one extra tag for it — protection or descriptive. |
| **Triage** | Once per conflict, treat a person or machine: clear 1 strain, or downgrade one condition (hard → normal, conflict → momentary). |
| **Structural Eye** | After real time inside a structure, vessel, or machine, roll Mental against 3. Win: you know its layout, its load-bearing weak point, and one way to bring it down or hold it up. Loss: the layout only — and one thing you assume is wrong. |
| **Overbuilt** | What you make lasts. Choose one: your Jury-Rigs survive past the conflict, your repairs become permanent without downtime, or one thing you build carries a tag beyond your genre's norm. |
| **Field of Fire** | When you have time to prepare ground — traps, barricades, firing lines, wards — allies who fight from your works gain an edge on their first contest there, and the ground itself may carry a condition for enemies who cross it. |

## Callings

### The Artificer — *makes the future*

Your workshop is where the setting changes.

**Signature — Works of Wonder:** on project countdowns you personally lead, even your losses strike 1 segment — genius profits from failure. Once per campaign arc, propose a wonder that bends the setting's rules — an airship, a vaccine, an ansible, a moving fortress — as a faction project.

### The Siegewright — *raises walls and unmakes them*

Fortification and demolition are the same craft read in two directions.

**Signature — Immovable / Irresistible:** a holding or position you fortify gains a standing protection tag its defenders can spend. And once per conflict, when your goal is to breach a structure, vehicle, or fortification, treat its defenses as one tier weaker — you know exactly where it wants to fail.

### The Mechanic — *keeps it running*

Engines, reactors, looms, and the great machines nobody else understands.

**Signature — Nurse It Along:** you may operate or repair a machine that by rights should not still function — broken, sabotaged, out of fuel, half-built. Once per conflict, ignore one condition or missing part on a machine you tend for a single, crucial contest, then it fails again if you stop.
