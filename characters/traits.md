# Gifts & Flaws

The Party has no ancestries and no race features. Instead, every character takes **two gifts** and **one or two flaws** — traits open to anyone, in any genre, that live halfway between rules and story. A gift can be a body, a history, a knack, or a blessing; the mechanical text is fixed, but what it *looks like* is yours. *Night Eyes* might be elf blood, cybernetic pupils, a cave-born childhood, or a saint's favor.

## Gifts

Pick two. Each is deliberately narrow — gifts should change *how* you solve problems, not just make you better at everything.

| Gift | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Iron Frame** | You carry a personal protection tag (shrug off 1 strain, once per scene) — thick skin, dense bone, or sheer stubbornness. |
| **Sixth Sense** | You cannot be surprised, and you gain an edge on noticing ambushes and hidden danger. |
| **Polyglot** | You speak, read, or interface with every language and code your genre plausibly allows, and gain an edge on decoding the ones that resist. |
| **Striking** | Gain an edge on first-impression social contests. People remember you — for good and ill. |
| **Steady Hands** | Gain an edge on delicate, precise work under pressure. |
| **Big** | Gain an edge when raw size and strength decide the matter, and the fiction treats you as a crowd's worth of muscle. |
| **Small** | Gain an edge when slipping through, hiding within, or being overlooked. |
| **Silver Memory** | Perfect recall. Ask the GM to truthfully re-describe any scene you witnessed. |
| **Night Eyes** | Darkness and poor visibility never impose setbacks on you. |
| **Charmed Life** | Once per session, reroll one of your dice for free. |
| **Old Friends Everywhere** | Once per session, you know someone here — a minor contact, established as fact. |
| **Fast** | Gain an edge in chases, and shift two range bands where others shift one, when unopposed. |
| **Unshakeable** | Gain an edge resisting fear, coercion, and mind-bending, and morale conditions on you downgrade one step (hard → normal, scene → momentary). |
| **Born to the Trade** | Pick a craft, vehicle, or instrument. Gain an edge when using it in its element. |
| **Lucky Star** | Once per session, after a costly success, cancel the cost. The complication simply… doesn't. |

## Flaws

Pick one — or two, if you want a stormier life and faster growth. A flaw is a standing invitation for trouble:

> **When a flaw materially complicates your situation, the complication happens — and you gain 1 perk.** Either you or the GM may propose a flaw's bite; the table agrees it's a real cost, not set dressing. Maximum one perk per flaw per session.

Flaws are the engine of the [perk economy](characters/advancement.md): the characters who suffer most colorfully advance fastest. This is intended.

| Flaw | The trouble it invites |
| --- | --- |
| **Notorious** | Your face or name is known — and recognized at the worst possible times. |
| **Bad Blood** | An enemy, inside the faction or out, with reach and a grudge. |
| **Vice** | A hunger — drink, gambling, adrenaline, approval — that picks its moments badly. |
| **Proud** | You cannot let an insult, challenge, or slight pass unanswered. |
| **Haunted** | A past (or a literal ghost) that surfaces uninvited. |
| **Old Wound** | Your body fails at bad times — when it flares, take a setback on the Physical contest at hand. |
| **True Believer** | Your conviction cannot bend, even when it should. The creed decides, not you. |
| **Doubter** | You don't fully believe in the cause — and it shows at the worst moments. |
| **Soft Heart** | You cannot abandon the vulnerable, even when the mission says walk away. |
| **Greedy** | The score matters more than the plan. It always has. |
| **Wanted** | An authority actively hunts you, with resources. |
| **Loose Tongue** | Secrets don't keep in your mouth. |

## The Faction Has One Too

During faction creation, the table picks a **faction flaw** — *Fanatical, Infiltrated, Indebted, Bloody Reputation, Schism-Prone, Overextended*. It works the same way: when it materially complicates a venture or faction turn, the trouble happens, and one involved player takes 1 perk. The organization's dysfunction is everyone's story fuel.

## Writing New Traits

Gifts and flaws are the easiest place to homebrew. Keep to the template:

- A **gift** grants one narrow edge, one narrow permission, or one once-per-session trick. Never a flat pool bonus, never "+1 to everything social."
- A **flaw** names a trouble specific enough to schedule and broad enough to recur. If you can't imagine it biting twice per arc, sharpen it.
