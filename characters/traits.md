# Gifts & Flaws

> *"Everyone worth a story has a gift and everyone worth a story has a crack. Show me one with neither and I'll show you the scenery."*
> — a playwright of the Vael court, twice exiled for accuracy

The Party has no ancestries and no race features. Instead, every character takes **two gifts and one flaw** — traits open to anyone, in any genre. The mechanical text of each is fixed; what it looks like is yours. *Night Eyes* might be elf blood, cybernetic pupils, a cave-born childhood, or a saint's favor.

Gifts and flaws are mirror images. A gift grants an edge on a narrow slice of life; a flaw imposes a [snag](core/contests.md#edges-and-snags) on one. Both are permanent, both are visible on your sheet, and both should come up every few sessions — if one never does, sharpen it with the GM.

## Gifts

Pick two. Each is deliberately narrow — a gift changes *how* you solve problems, not how good you are at everything.

| Gift | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Acrobat** | Edge on climbing, leaping, tumbling, and squeezing through where bodies shouldn't fit. |
| **Beastwise** | Edge on handling, calming, or reading animals, mounts, and beasts — whatever counts as one in your setting. |
| **Big** | Edge when raw size and strength decide the matter; the narrative treats you as a crowd's worth of muscle. |
| **Born to the Trade** | Pick a craft, vehicle, or instrument. Edge when using it in its element. |
| **Cool Head** | Edge when acting under time pressure — the fuse lit, the alarm ringing, the water rising. |
| **Ear for Lies** | Edge on telling truth from performance, face to face. |
| **Fast** | Edge in chases; when movement is unopposed, you shift two range bands where others shift one. |
| **Iron Frame** | You carry a personal protection tag (negate 1 strain, once per conflict) — thick skin, dense bone, or sheer stubbornness. |
| **Mimic** | Edge on impersonating voices, manners, and bearing — passing as someone, or someone's kind. |
| **Night Eyes** | Darkness and poor visibility never snag you. |
| **Polyglot** | You speak, read, or interface with every language and code your genre plausibly allows; edge on decoding the ones that resist. |
| **Reckoner** | Edge on rapid figuring — odds, values, loads, distances, what the cargo is really worth. |
| **Silver Memory** | Perfect recall. Ask the GM to truthfully re-describe any scene you witnessed. |
| **Sixth Sense** | You cannot be surprised; edge on noticing ambushes and hidden danger. |
| **Small** | Edge when slipping through, hiding within, or being overlooked. |
| **Steady Hands** | Edge on delicate, precise work under pressure. |
| **Striking** | Edge on first-impression social contests. People remember you — for good and ill. |
| **Tireless** | Edge on contests of endurance — the forced march, the long watch, the third night without sleep. |
| **Unshakeable** | Edge resisting fear, coercion, and mind-bending; morale conditions on you downgrade one step (hard → normal, conflict → momentary). |
| **Weathered** | Storm, heat, cold, thin air — hostile environments never snag you. |

## Flaws

Take one at creation. A flaw is a gift in reverse: a standing snag on a narrow slice of life, and a standing invitation for the GM to make that slice matter. When a flaw's snag plausibly applies to a contest, it applies — the same test as a condition, applied by the same table.

| Flaw | The snag |
| --- | --- |
| **Bad Blood** | An enemy with reach and a grudge. Snag on any contest their reach plausibly touches — their turf, their people, their money. |
| **Doubter** | You don't fully believe. Snag on Ideological contests where the cause itself is supposed to carry you. |
| **Greedy** | Snag on walking past a score, splitting fairly, or spending where there's no return. |
| **Haunted** | A past that surfaces uninvited. When its reminders are present, snag on contests of focus and composure. |
| **Loose Tongue** | Snag on keeping a secret under any social pressure — drink, flattery, fury, or fun. |
| **Notorious** | Your face or name travels ahead of you. Snag on going unnoticed, passing as harmless, or denying who you are. |
| **Old Wound** | Name it at creation. Snag on the Physical contests it plausibly bites — the sprint, the climb, the cold night. |
| **Proud** | Snag on backing down, letting a slight pass, or playing smaller than you are. |
| **Soft Heart** | Snag on acting against the vulnerable, or on any goal that means leaving them behind. |
| **True Believer** | The creed decides, not you. Snag on compromising, deceiving in the creed's name, or acting against doctrine. |
| **Vice** | Name the hunger — drink, gambling, adrenaline, approval. Snag on resisting, delaying, or walking past it. |
| **Wanted** | An authority hunts you. Snag on moving openly anywhere it watches. |

Flaws don't pay you by the incident. They make you specific — and specific characters are the ones the story bends around. GMs: when a flaw genuinely costs a player something, remember it when [perks](characters/advancement.md#earning-perks) go around.

### Scars Are Flaws

When you're [broken under mortal stakes](core/strain-and-conditions.md#broken) and choose to survive, you take a scar — written as a new flaw, using the same template: a name, a snag scope, an invitation. *Ruined Knee* snags the sprint and the ladder. *Dead to My Family* snags every contest where their world touches yours. Scars accumulate; a long career reads like a map of what it cost.

## The Faction's Gifts & Flaws

The faction takes **one gift and one flaw** of its own during [faction creation](faction/overview.md#faction-creation-session-zero). Same logic, organizational scale.

### Faction Gifts

| Gift | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Beloved** | Edge on contests to rally, recruit, or reassure its own people; rival influence aimed at them takes a snag. |
| **Deep Pockets** | Once per faction turn, treat 1 stock as [Ready](faction/nodes.md#accessibility) regardless of where it sits — somebody stashed exactly the right thing. |
| **Endowed** | Begin play with 3 extra stock, placed anywhere on the map. |
| **Old Names** | Edge on contests where legitimacy, tradition, or pedigree carries weight. |
| **Everywhere** | Its people turn up. Once per session, a friendly member plausibly exists in the scene — a face in the crowd, a clerk behind the counter. |
| **Tight-Lipped** | Rival Spy actions against the faction take a snag. |
| **War-Tested** | Edge on contests to defend its own places and people. |
| **Zealous** | Recruit actions gain an edge; new members arrive already believing. |

### Faction Flaws

| Flaw | The snag |
| --- | --- |
| **Bloody Reputation** | Snag on diplomacy, parley, and any contest that needs strangers to assume good faith. |
| **Fanatical** | Snag on compromise, restraint, and half-measures — the faction only knows one speed. |
| **Hidebound** | Snag on adopting new methods, tools, doctrine, or allies. |
| **Indebted** | Someone owns a piece of you. Add 1 stock owed to the faction's [Keep](faction/faction-turn.md#2--keep) every faction turn until the debt is resolved in play. |
| **Infiltrated** | Someone inside reports to someone outside. Rival Spy actions against you gain an edge until the leak is found. |
| **Overextended** | The faction's Keep includes one need its regular income can't cover. Every turn, something goes hungry unless play provides. |
| **Schism-Prone** | Old fault lines. Snag on Quell actions, and Cohesion losses from internal strife cost 1 extra. |
| **Watched** | An authority keeps a file. Snag on the faction operating openly anywhere that authority reaches. |

## Writing New Traits

Gifts and flaws are the easiest place to homebrew. Keep the template:

- A **gift** grants one narrow edge, one narrow permission, or one once-per-conflict trick. Never a flat pool bonus, never "+1 to everything social."
- A **flaw** imposes one narrow snag with teeth — specific enough to see coming, broad enough to recur. If it can't plausibly bite twice per story arc, sharpen it.
