# Classes — The Seven Offices

A class in The Party is an **office**: the role you hold in the faction's living body. The seven offices are named for the body because every organization, in every genre, has the same anatomy — something that decides, something that speaks, something that strikes, something that watches, something that knows, something that binds, and something that carries.

| Office | Role | Archetypes across genres |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [**The Head**](characters/classes/head.md) | Command & decision | captain, high priest, don, cell leader, CEO, warband chief |
| [**The Voice**](characters/classes/voice.md) | Image & persuasion | propagandist, diplomat, media star, herald, hype man, comms officer |
| [**The Hand**](characters/classes/hand.md) | Force & protection | champion, enforcer, marine, duelist, bodyguard, gunslinger |
| [**The Eye**](characters/classes/eye.md) | Secrets & infiltration | spy, scout, hacker, inquisitor, second-story artist, sensor officer |
| [**The Mind**](characters/classes/mind.md) | Expertise & the impossible | engineer, wizard, surgeon, forger, alchemist, systems operator |
| [**The Heart**](characters/classes/heart.md) | Morale & cohesion | chaplain, medic, quartermaster of spirits, bard, counselor, cook |
| [**The Spine**](characters/classes/spine.md) | Supply & administration | logistician, fence, purser, smuggler, steward, guildmaster of coin |

## How a Class Is Built

Every class has the same chassis:

- **Core feature** — an always-available signature ability that defines the office. Usable from session one; you never wait to be cool.
- **Feature list** — six features unique to the class. You start with two and buy more with [advances](characters/advancement.md).
- **Three callings** — subclasses that angle the office toward a style. Each grants a signature feature at creation and colors how you play. *(This draft ships one feature per calling; deeper calling tiers are handled by the [Deepen rule](characters/advancement.md#deepening) — see Open Questions.)*
- **Faction synergy** — a standing bonus the office grants during the [faction turn](faction/faction-turn.md). This is what makes every character organizationally load-bearing, and it stacks (gently) if two players share the office.

## Design Rules for Classes

These are the constraints the seven classes were written under — and the constraints any homebrew class should follow:

1. **Role, not day job.** A class must make sense for a priest, a pilot, and a pickpocket simultaneously. If a feature only works with swords or spells, it's mis-scoped.
2. **No gates.** Anything a class does *well*, anyone can attempt. Features grant edges, extra uses, and unique twists — never exclusive permission to participate in a pillar of play.
3. **Both scales.** Every class must matter in a knife fight *and* in a faction turn. The synergy line guarantees the latter; at least two features per class should reach personal-scale conflict.
4. **Small numbers.** Features grant edges, rerolls, ticks, stock, and conditions. They never grant flat bonuses, extra dice beyond the 5-cap, or bigger tracks (one exception, and it's the Hand's whole identity).
5. **Combos over buttons.** Features should create set-ups other players can pay off — The Party's answer to the "cool builds" of D&D is cross-*player* combos, not solo rotations.

## Multiclassing

When you buy a new feature with an advance, you may buy from **another class's list at +1 perk cost**, provided the fiction supports your character genuinely working that office (train with the Spine, shadow the Eye). Core features and calling signatures are never cross-buyable — offices can be learned; being *the* Head cannot.

You may also formally **change office** during downtime if the faction's fiction supports it (a promotion, a demotion, a coup). Swap core features; keep bought features; your old calling signature becomes a normal feature.
