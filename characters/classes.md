# The Nine Seats

A class in The Party is a **Seat**: the chair you hold in the faction's inner circle. Every organization, in every genre, has the same anatomy — something that decides, something that speaks, something that strikes, something that watches, something that knows, something that makes, something that ranges out, something that binds, and something that carries. The Seats are named for that anatomy.

| [**The Mouth**](characters/classes/mouth.md) | Image & persuasion | propagandist, diplomat, media star, herald, hype man, comms officer |
| [**The Fist**](characters/classes/fist.md) | Force & protection | champion, enforcer, marine, duelist, bodyguard, gunslinger |
| [**The Eye**](characters/classes/eye.md) | Secrets & intelligence | spymaster, analyst, inquisitor, handler, sensor officer |
| [**The Heel**](characters/classes/heel.md) | Movement & reconnaissance | scout, ranger, outrider, pilot, navigator, messenger |
| [**The Mind**](characters/classes/mind.md) | Knowledge & foresight | sage, scholar, scribe, strategist, seer, savant |

## How a Seat Is Built

Every Seat has the same chassis:

- **Core feature** — the signature ability that defines the Seat, available from session one.
- **Feature list** — six features unique to the Seat. You start with two and buy more with [advances](characters/advancement.md).
- **Three callings** — styles within the Seat. Each grants a signature feature at creation and colors how you play.
- **Faction synergy** — a standing benefit the Seat grants during the [faction turn](faction/faction-turn.md): an edge on the kind of action the Seat is built for, and, when two players share the Seat, an extra stock of one pool at Income. The eight non-Head Seats each feed a different one of the [eight pools](faction/resources.md), so a broad table quietly enriches its whole economy — while the Head, who feeds no pool, grants the faction an extra action instead.

## Design Rules for Seats

The constraints the nine were written under, and the constraints any homebrew Seat should follow:

1. **Role, not day job.** A Seat must make sense for a priest, a pilot, and a pickpocket at once. If a feature only works with swords or spells, it's mis-scoped.
2. **No gates.** Anything a Seat does well, anyone can attempt. Features grant edges, extra uses, and unique twists — never exclusive permission to participate in a whole pillar of play.
3. **Both scales.** Every Seat matters in a knife fight and in a faction turn. The synergy line guarantees the latter; at least two features per Seat reach personal conflict.
4. **Small numbers.** Features grant edges, rerolls, countdown segments, stock, and conditions — never flat bonuses, dice past the 5-cap, or extra strain boxes (one exception: the Fist's Bulwark).
5. **The dice stay live.** Features shift probabilities; they don't delete them. Anything that "just happens" should be narrow, mundane, and dramatic never — the moment a feature would decide a scene, there's a roll in it.
6. **Combos over buttons.** Features should create set-ups other players can pay off. The Party's answer to character builds is cross-*player* combos.

## Learning Other Seats

When you buy a new feature with an advance, you may buy from **another Seat's list at +1 perk**, provided the narrative shows your character genuinely learning that trade — training with the Spine, shadowing the Eye. Core features and calling signatures are never cross-buyable. A Seat's craft can be learned; being *the* Head cannot.
